<p style="color:#AAAAAA; font-size:13px; font-weight:normal; text-align:center; margin-top:0.0em; margin-bottom:6.0em; ">Fan Fiction — Not for Commercial Distribution</p>

<p style="font-size:24px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:2.0em; margin-bottom:0.5em; border-bottom: 2px solid #8B2500; padding-bottom: 0.5em;">What This Document Is</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The series bible covers the scaffold. This document covers everything underneath it. The why behind every who. The feeling behind every scene. The decisions made in development that never appear as stage directions but shape every line of dialogue.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">If the series bible is the skeleton, this is the nervous system.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Anyone writing for Firefly: Legacy should read this before writing a single line. It is a living document. Every commit to the episode drafts triggers an update here.</p>

<p style="font-size:24px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:2.0em; margin-bottom:0.5em; border-bottom: 2px solid #8B2500; padding-bottom: 0.5em;">Canon Sources — What We Use and What We Don’t</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">This project is built on specific canon sources. Not all Firefly-adjacent material is treated equally. This note exists so that readers and future writers know exactly what ground this story stands on.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">CANONICAL FOR THIS PROJECT:</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The original Firefly television series (2002-2003, Fox / Mutant Enemy). All fourteen produced episodes including the unaired pilot. This is the primary source for character voices, verse detail, and tone.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Serenity (2005, Universal Pictures). The feature film. All events in the film are canon for this project. The deaths of Wash and Book are canon. Miranda is canon. The Operative is canon.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The Shepherd’s Tale (2010, Dark Horse Comics). Written by Joss Whedon and Zack Whedon. Book’s full backstory — Henry Evans, the Alliance mole, the four thousand fatalities — is drawn from this source and treated as hard canon.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Serenity: Float Out (2010, Dark Horse Comics). Zoe’s pregnancy is noted from this source. Treated as canon.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Serenity: Leaves on the Wind (2014, Dark Horse Comics). Written by Zack Whedon. Set nine months after the film. CRITICAL TIMELINE NOTE: In this comic Jayne has left Serenity to settle with his mother. He left because Mal planned to go into hiding after Miranda and Jayne is a man of action — that didn’t sit well with him. This is our canon departure point for Jayne leaving Mal’s crew.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">This has significant implications for our story. Jayne left Serenity approximately nine months after the film. Saff was born sometime in the year or two following the film — meaning Jayne left when Saff was either not yet born or a newborn infant. He would not know her. He would not recognise her in the vault in episode one. He recognises the silk scarf because he knew Inara’s scarf. He recognises something around Cas’s eyes as Wash. He does not know who these kids are in the vault. He figures it out on the ship across the first days together.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">This makes the vault scene more powerful not less — he lowers his gun on instinct before he knows why. And it makes the mess room in episode five more powerful — Jayne has been piecing it together for weeks, sitting on it, and now here they are.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">NOT CANONICAL FOR THIS PROJECT:</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">All-New Firefly (2022-present, BOOM Studios). This ongoing comic series under a different creative team depicts events including Jayne’s death and other developments that directly contradict our story. It is not used and should not be referenced. In our verse Jayne Cobb is alive at sixty one years old and has been for the entire season.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Any other expanded universe material not listed above should be treated with caution. When in doubt, check the original series and film first. If the source is not Whedon-credited Dark Horse material, verify before using.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">THE RULE:</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">When canon research returns results from All-New Firefly or BOOM Studios material, discard them. Our Jayne is not dead. Our verse diverges from that timeline. The story you are reading is one possible future after Serenity. BOOM Studios is a different one. Both can exist. Only one is ours.</p>

<p style="font-size:24px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:2.0em; margin-bottom:0.5em; border-bottom: 2px solid #8B2500; padding-bottom: 0.5em;">Tone Rules</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">What This Story Is</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Funny until it’s heartbreaking</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The core Firefly formula. Comedy and gut-punch often in the same sentence. The stegosaurus scene only works because nothing before it signals it’s coming.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Silence does as much work as dialogue</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The most devastating moments are often stage directions. Jayne lowering his gun. Saff almost smiling. Pip turning back to his stew. Trust the pause.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Nobody makes a speech</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Even emotional moments are oblique and slightly defensive. The moment a character starts making a speech the scene has gone wrong.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The ship is always a character</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Serenity groans and sighs and the crew responds to her like she’s alive. She is not a vehicle. She is the heart of the story.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Verse is lived in and tired</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Nothing is clean. Nothing is new. Ships are held together by tape and stubbornness.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The antagonist watches from comfort</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The Operator runs his operation from a command center. He is never in physical danger. That contrast with the crews scrambling is deliberate.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">What This Story Is Not</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Not a reunion show. Not a nostalgia exercise. Not clean or safe. People die. Plans fail. The right call still costs something.</p>

<p style="font-size:24px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:2.0em; margin-bottom:0.5em; border-bottom: 2px solid #8B2500; padding-bottom: 0.5em;">Character Context</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:24px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.5em; margin-bottom:0.5em; ">SAFF REYNOLDS   |   Captain / Wildcard   |   Age 24</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Inheritance</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">From Mal: the instinct to do the right thing even when expensive. The stubbornness. The brown coat. From Inara: the ability to read a room before she robs it. The silk scarf over a tactical vest — both parents in one image.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">How She Was Raised — The Full Backstory</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Mal couldn’t raise a child on Serenity the way it was. So he made a deal. A Companion stays with them. Saff gets trained properly in the Guild’s arts. She grows up knowing their world from the inside. Practical. Very Mal. He’d never admit it was also him trying to give her something of her mother.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Saff grew up in rooms of silk and learned everything the Guild teaches. How to walk into a space. How to read it before she robs it. How to carry herself so that dangerous people feel safe long enough for the situation to change. She absorbed it all without meaning to. She has spent her whole life resisting what she absorbed. It surfaces anyway.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Then the moment arrived — the final rite of passage every Companion faces when their training is complete. The Guild considers it the proof that you are ready. It is not malicious. It is not forced. It is a test. Saff passed it. She looked at what was being asked of her, understood exactly what it meant, and chose differently. That is not failure. That is exactly the kind of person Mal Reynolds raised his daughter to be. He knew the moment was coming from the day he made the arrangement. He helped her walk away not because she wasn’t ready — but because she was.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Mal then taught her everything else. How to fight. How to survive. How to be hard enough to last in the verse. She carries both inheritances. The silk scarf and the tactical vest. Both parents in one image — but now we understand exactly how she got there.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Inara — The Full Picture</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">After the events of Serenity and the Miranda broadcast, Inara was expelled from the Companion Guild. She was also ill — a rare terminal blood condition. But she found a cure, or was cured, and in the years that followed she rose through the Guild’s political structure to become High Priestess of House Madrassa.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">She stayed away from Saff not because she was captive or dying. She stayed away to protect her. A High Priestess with enemies is a target. Anyone close to her is leverage. Saff growing up unknown, unconnected, off the Guild’s radar — that was Inara’s choice. The hardest one she ever made.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The note in the box says: if I’m not in your life, here’s where to find me. And here’s why I stayed away.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The Guild is a viper’s nest. They trade in information and political leverage as much as the Shepherds did — more so. Inara has enemies within it who know about her past with Mal and would use it. Finding her means going through them.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Companion Training as an Unconscious Resource</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The training surfaces across the season in moments of diplomacy Saff frames as tactics. The vault scanner scene — Pip tells her to think nice thoughts, she drops her shoulders half an inch, walks through clean. The silk scarf recognised by a Companion in episode five before she recognises Saff. The specific way she can make dangerous people feel safe long enough for the situation to change.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">It is never named as a skill. It is always instinct. The reader understands what it actually is.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Jayne Question — Does She Know Him</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Jayne left Mal’s crew approximately nine months after the Serenity film — canon from Leaves on the Wind. Saff was born sometime in the year or two after the film. Jayne left when she was either not yet born or a newborn. He does not know her. She does not know him.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">In the vault in episode one Jayne does not recognise Saff. He recognises the silk scarf — he knew Inara’s scarf. He recognises something around Cas’s eyes as Wash. He pieces together who these kids are over the days on the ship. He sits on it. Says nothing. Gives it up only when he has to.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Saff has no memory of Jayne. She was an infant when he left. When he steps out of the shadows in the vault she sees a very large old man with a gun and a hat. Nothing more. Not yet.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Arc</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Stops running from the inheritance and starts owning it. The coat confirms what she already is. By the time Mal hands it over she has been captain for nine episodes.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Voice</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Economical. Precise. Minimum required words. Steers conversations without appearing to. One tell: when genuinely surprised her response is one beat slower than usual.</p>

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:14px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; border-bottom: 2px solid #8B2500; padding-bottom: 0.5em;">Key decision: Saff refuses to leave Cas in the vault. Mal’s daughter making Mal’s choice without knowing it.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:24px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.5em; margin-bottom:0.5em; ">CAS WASHBURNE   |   Pilot   |   Age 23</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Inheritance</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">From Wash: the instinct, the feel for a ship, the self-deprecating humour, the Hawaiian shirt. From Zoe: the tactical brain, the stillness under pressure, the weight of responsibility for her crew.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Conflict</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Makes the right tactical call. Something human and unpredictable happens anyway. Turns it inward immediately. Should have seen that. Should have been better. Leaf on the wind is not chaos — it’s reading every current so precisely it looks effortless. Cas hasn’t got there yet.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Crew — Episode One</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">DECKER — fifties, ex-military, clicks the safety before anyone asks. His death sits heaviest because the plan was right and he died anyway. LENA — captured not killed. An ongoing debt. REX — runs. Cas vouched for her. That misjudgement is quiet and permanent.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Photograph</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Taped to the Eleanor’s console, slightly crooked. Zoe in military gear. Nobody has asked. In episode two Cas compares the Eleanor’s chair to Serenity’s chair. The Eleanor is already a ghost.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Arc</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Learning to trust the adjustment. Her father teaches her from a recording. From beyond the grave.</p>

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:14px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; border-bottom: 2px solid #8B2500; padding-bottom: 0.5em;">Key decision: Cas is the bounty. Finding out she was the target — alone, mid-standoff — lands as a very specific tired resignation. Very Wash. That look is the first thing that makes Saff hesitate.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:24px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.5em; margin-bottom:0.5em; ">PIP TAM   |   Mechanic   |   Age 24</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Inheritance</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">From Kaylee: the warmth, the enthusiasm, the inability to let a struggling engine suffer in silence. From Simon: the precision, the methodical diagnosis, the very high standard he holds himself to.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Two Modes</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">In public: pure Kaylee. In the engine room: Simon surfaces. Methodical, precise, but with Kaylee’s joy underneath. The Serenity engine room is the first time both modes run simultaneously.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Critical Detail</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Pip is a generalist. He does not fall in love with Fireflies. He falls in love with this one specifically. His expression doesn’t change when he reads the nameplate. That is the tell.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Maintenance Notes</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">On day three of episode two, Pip finds Kaylee’s hand-written maintenance notes behind the primary coupling housing. He reads three pages and has to stop. He doesn’t tell anyone. Thread opens later in the season.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Kaylee’s Backstory</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Kaylee suffered severe abdominal trauma in Serenity — the Reaver harpoon, emergency surgery in rough conditions. The damage complicated or prevented natural pregnancy. Simon spent two years finding a solution. Pip exists because his father wouldn’t accept what the Verse broke.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Plant: Kaylee at the medical centre — ‘Doctor said it wasn’t possible you know. Simon said different.’ She doesn’t know she’s talking to Pip. Payoff: Jayne sideways, much later — ‘Your father spent two years fixing something the Verse broke.’</p>

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:14px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; border-bottom: 2px solid #8B2500; padding-bottom: 0.5em;">Key decision: The correction of Cole must never play as a put-down. Pip genuinely could not help himself. The spoon gesture is involuntary.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:24px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.5em; margin-bottom:0.5em; ">JAYNE COBB   |   The Anchor   |   Age 61</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Why Jayne Works As The Regular</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Always the outsider. Bridges generations without belonging to either. Not a hero to the new crew. Just the man who won’t get off their ship.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">What Twenty Years Has Done</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Slower. Compensated by being smarter about positioning. Still hits harder than men half his age. The discovery that the kids are who they are cracks something open that he spends the season trying to close.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Truth He Carries</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Knows what happened to the original crew. Knows the real versions of the stories. Will not volunteer this. Gives it up piece by piece only when withholding becomes unconscionable.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Carving</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">His name in the mess table. Crude letters. Decades old. Pip runs his finger along it in episode two. Jayne taps it once with a knife blade. No words. That tap is his entire autobiography on this ship.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:24px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.5em; margin-bottom:0.5em; ">DAVE   |   The Broker — Rattle Rock Station   |   Age —</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">What He Is</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Distinction From Mr Universe</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Double Black Box Arc</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Episode 3: The crew take an extraction job from Dave. Job goes south. <p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Episodes 4-5: The box is gone. They don’t think about it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Episode 7: Someone — Pip, probably, Simon-mode, methodical — starts working on the first box and hits a wall. <p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The moment: ‘Oh.’ Quiet. To nobody. Then he gets up and goes to find the others.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Dave’s reaction when he understands what he’s been sitting on: ‘Oh. The homage lands here, not in the equipment or the setup, but in the specific moment of a man who trades in information understanding for the first time that some information is too heavy to hold.</p>

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:14px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; border-bottom: 2px solid #8B2500; padding-bottom: 0.5em;">Key decision: Dave cracks the second box but doesn’t understand what he’s found until the crew come back. The homage to Mr Universe lives in that moment of realisation — not in the character’s setup but in the emotional beat. Enough to feel it. Not so much that it becomes a copy.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:24px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.5em; margin-bottom:0.5em; ">THE OPERATOR   |   Alliance Antagonist   |   Age —</p>

<p style="font-size:24px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:2.0em; margin-bottom:0.5em; border-bottom: 2px solid #8B2500; padding-bottom: 0.5em;">Episode Structure Notes</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">They land. Meet Dave. <p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Saff is furious. No pay. Jayne looks at the box. Says nothing.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Episode Four — LEAF ON THE WIND — IN DEVELOPMENT</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">No job. No action. Log one stops her cold — she takes a break, goes to the galley, Pip is there, neither of them says anything. Then she goes back and plays the rest.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The four logs are a pilot’s guide to Serenity. Practical. Technical. For whoever comes after. But the voice evolves across them exactly as Wash evolved — early funny and unbothered, middle heavier but still wry, late quiet and almost still.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Log One — Early Days on Serenity</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Canon anchor: Early series. Wash young, calm, completely at home. The deadpan competent pilot from the pilot episode. Holding a pterodactyl. Doesn’t notice he’s holding it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Flying content: How she pulls left on a hard burn. Her sensors lying in atmo below four thousand feet. The sigh before a hard restart — when you hear it everything is fine, she’s just clearing her throat. Trust your hands over the board.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Tone: Pure early Wash. Funny, practical, unbothered. Ends with him catching himself quoting the wrong dinosaur and stopping. ‘Wrong dinosaur.’ To himself. Shaking his head.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Log Two — After Out of Gas</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Canon anchor: Serenity loses engine power completely. Wash stays at Zoe’s side refusing to leave while she’s unconscious. Mal drags him back to the helm. He has to fly blind — dark board, no instruments, just waiting for Mal to restart the engine from the other end of a dying ship.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Flying content: What to do when the board goes dark. Flying without instruments in extremis. Trusting your hands over everything. Getting smaller not bigger when it goes worst.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Tone: Still Wash. Still funny in places. But the humour sits next to something heavier now. He never mentions Zoe. Talks entirely about instrument failure and hand-flying. But every word of it is about sitting at a dead helm next to his unconscious wife and waiting.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Log Three — After War Stories</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Canon anchor: Wash demanded to go on a job out of jealousy. Got tortured by Niska alongside Mal. Kept up banter through the entire torture session. Was rescued by Zoe. His flying style oscillated between near-panic and Zen-like calm — most calm when facing the greatest danger.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Flying content: What to do when the situation is the problem not the ship. When panic wants the controls. The counterintuitive move — getting quieter not louder. He references the Crazy Ivan obliquely — ‘there was a moment once where the only move was the one that looked like suicide’ — without naming it. The technical content: when the options run out, what then.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Tone: Heavier. The gap between what flying looks like from the outside and what it actually is. He’s close to the leaf on the wind without having the words for it yet. Doesn’t name it. Just describes it from the inside.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Log Four — En Route to Mr Universe — Serenity Film</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Canon anchor: Recorded on the way in. Mal has told the crew what they’re flying into and why. Everyone knows the odds. Wash is alone in the cockpit pointed at Reaver space. He sits down and records one more log not because he’s planning to die but because he has something left to say and the verse is about to get very loud.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">IMPORTANT TIMELINE NOTE: Cannot be set after Miranda — the film’s finale is non-stop action from the moment they enter the ion cloud to Wash’s death. There is no gap. This log is recorded before they go in, not after.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Flying content: Flying damaged. What to do when the ship is hurt and you need more from her than she has left to give. Specific and practical. He’s talking about what’s about to happen without knowing it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Tone: Short. Quiet. The shortest log. Somewhere in it the words come out almost by themselves: ‘I am a leaf on the wind.’ He pauses. Considers it. ‘I don’t entirely know what that means. But it feels right.’ He reaches forward. Ends the log. That’s the last thing Wash ever recorded. Cas plays it twice.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Episode Five — TBC — SCAFFOLDED</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Saff reads the note in the post credits of episode four. She knows where her mother is. Getting there means going through the Companion Guild on Sihnon. She doesn’t hesitate.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Saff’s Two Fighting Styles — Canon Confirmed</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Companions are trained in unarmed martial arts, fencing, dance, psychology, and self-defence including skin-contact sedatives. Inara specifically is adept at swordplay, martial arts, and the bow. Saff received all of this training through Mal’s arrangement with the teacher Inara sent. Mal then taught her everything else — firearms, tactics, the verse’s version of street fighting. She carries both. Nobody who trained her finished the job the way they intended.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Scene One — Setting Course</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Saff finds Cas. Orders a heading to Sihnon. No explanation. Cas sets the course because that’s what she does. Jayne walks in, clocks the heading, and says exactly what he thinks. Saff listens to all of it. Tells him nobody needs a part in it. She takes the shuttle. She goes alone.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Scene Two — The Approach</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Sihnon almost too bright. Too deliberate. The shuttle undocks. Saff arranges the silk scarf properly for the first time since she was a child. The transponder pings. A voice comes back immediately: Serenity shuttle, you are cleared for landing. Berth four. Welcome to House Madrassa. Saff doesn’t respond. Sets her jaw. Brings the shuttle down.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Yusara — The Rival</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Senior House Priestess of House Madrassa. Perhaps sixty. The bearing of someone who has been comfortable in rooms of power for a very long time. She expected to be High Priestess herself before Inara rose above her. She has been waiting for this moment for years. She presents herself as entirely civilised and reasonable. She is. That’s what makes her dangerous. Her name is Yusara. Guild naming convention — Arabic in origin, fitting with House Madrassa. She stands down when Inara’s people arrive and the arithmetic reverses. She will be back. Everyone in the room knows it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">She steps out into the open grounds of House Madrassa — not a grand hall, a garden. Cloistered. Contemplative. Companions moving through it unhurried. Canon confirms the Guild houses are similar to monasteries — heavy religious overtones, incense, contemplation. A junior Companion approaches, warm, welcoming, following standing orders given a long time ago. She doesn’t know who Saff is. She just knows that if the Serenity shuttle ever arrived she was to bring the passenger somewhere specific. Not to Inara. Somewhere else first.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Saff knows it’s a trap. She walks into it anyway, head held high. She announces herself before the scarf is even properly clocked: My name is Saff Reynolds. Inara Serra is my mother. I imagine someone here has been expecting me. Not a demand. Not a request dressed as one. A statement of fact delivered in the Guild’s own register — the training doing its work whether she likes it or not. She makes it feel inevitable rather than aggressive. Then she waits. Patient. Lets the room come to her.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">She is led to tea with a senior House Priestess — a rival faction representative who has been waiting for this moment for years. Someone who expected to be High Priestess themselves before Inara rose above them. Senior enough to have Guild standing. Connected enough to have Alliance muscle. The tea is civilised. The conversation is careful. Saff reads everything and gives nothing away.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Scene Four — The Cell</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Mid-tea, armed guards appear. Alliance operatives or Guild security. Saff doesn’t fight. She’s outgunned and knows it. Fighting now would cost her the intelligence she came to gather. She goes quietly. She has already done what she came to do — mapped the trap, clocked the layout, identified the players. She thinks to herself: she’ll get out. Later.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Scene Five — The Demand</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The rival faction delivers their demand to Inara: stand down as High Priestess or your daughter dies. They allow time for the message to be delivered. They are confident. They do not know that Inara has her own intelligence network inside their organisation and has known exactly where Saff is from the moment she arrived.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Scene Six — Inara Comes</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Inara kits herself up. Canon confirms she is trained in martial arts, fencing, and is particularly skilled with a bow — she prefers it over firearms. She goes in quiet, precise, like a Japanese archer. Sword and bow. Twenty years of Guild politics has not made her soft. She knows every corridor, every guard rotation, every political lever. Alarms sound. They fight their way out together — Saff with her two fighting styles, Inara with hers. The rival’s people are routed.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Scene Seven — The Mother-Daughter Moment</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Once they are clear. No performance. No speech. Just two women who are more alike than either is comfortable with, catching their breath. Inara asks how she’s been since her final day of training — that’s when she lost track. Has she heard from her father. What has she been doing.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Not a warm reunion. Twenty years of absence doesn’t dissolve in one corridor. But something shifts. Something that was closed opens just enough.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Exit</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Serenity is in Core space and the wrong person seeing that ship means they are done. They must leave now. Inara says it plainly. Minutes not hours.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Dead Drop — New Melbourne</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">New Melbourne is a canon planet — mentioned in Objects in Space as a world Inara considered disembarking at. An oceanic border world. Not Core, not deep rim. Inara has plausible reason to pass through. Saff can reach it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The dead drop system: when a specific classified listing appears in New Melbourne’s public wave board — an ordinary advert for an ordinary service, the kind nobody watches — PO Box 1167 on New Melbourne has a letter in it. When the listing is gone the box is empty. Inara books the listing through the local paper. It cannot be intercepted. It is invisible because it is mundane.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">They part. Saff goes back to the shuttle. Inara disappears back into the house to manage the political fallout.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Shuttle Redocks</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The shuttle comes back. Saff redocks. She comes down the ramp. Cas, Pip, Jayne waiting. She doesn’t apologise. She thanks them for waiting. Then: she just had something to do. Jayne looks at her for a long moment. Doesn’t say anything. Turns and walks back inside. That’s enough.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Episode Six — TBC — SCAFFOLDED</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The one-liner: Someone gets hurt badly enough that Jayne knows a doctor — and the doctor turns out to be someone nobody in the room is ready to meet yet.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Scene One — The Wave</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The wave comes in for Cas at the helm. Her contact — someone she built a relationship with when she was starting out and working alone, the kind you earn through delivering not through referrals. Canon confirmed: waves are received and sent from the bridge. Wash operated communications from the pilot’s station throughout the series. Cas takes the call at the helm. Then calls the crew to the mess room. The briefing happens there with everyone present.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">They need money. They take a salvage job on a border world or dead moon — retrieving a cache of Browncoat war munitions, twenty-four years old, left behind when the Independents lost. Someone knows where they are. Someone wants them moved before the Alliance finds them and destroys them or uses them to track down anyone still connected to the Independent cause. Not theft. Not recovery of someone else’s property. Something that matters to people who can’t do it themselves.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Canon note: The Browncoats were given limited ammunition and supplemented their arsenals with whatever they could scrounge or steal from the Alliance. After the war most surviving soldiers became mercenaries. Some continued the conflict more directly. The munitions cache is a remnant of a war that didn’t fully end.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Complication</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The cache is being guarded by ageing Browncoat veterans who never stopped. They’re suspicious of everyone who shows up unannounced regardless of references. And they’re especially suspicious of a man old enough to have fought who didn’t. Jayne Cobb did not serve in the Unification War — canon confirmed. He was a mercenary before, during and after. The veterans clock this. Someone gets jumpy. Jayne takes the round covering the others getting out.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">He doesn’t go down immediately. He gets the crew out first. Then he goes down in the cargo bay once they’re back on the ship. He sits there bleeding and quiet for longer than he should before he tells Cas where to go. Because telling them where to go means admitting who is there.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Only Doctor He Trusts Is The Doctor He Hates</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Gut shot. Bad enough that the med kit won’t cover it. Bad enough that a back street doctor won’t do. Jayne knows one person. He gives the coordinates without explanation. Cas sets the course. On the way Pip asks where they’re going. Jayne doesn’t answer. Nobody pushes it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Simon Tam opens the door. He and Jayne look at each other across twenty years. Neither of them says anything useful for about thirty seconds. Then Simon steps back: get him inside. Because whatever else Simon Tam is he is a doctor first. Always has been. Even for Jayne.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">Simon and Kaylee — Where They Are</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Simon and Kaylee left Serenity around 2523 when Simon followed River. In 2542 Simon is approximately 51, Kaylee 45. They are settled on a border or rim world — not the Core, too much history there. The kind of place that doesn’t ask too many questions about where a doctor trained. Simon’s skills are genuinely needed there. They have built something real.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Kaylee Line</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">While Simon works, Kaylee is in the corner of the room pottering with something mechanical the way she always does — half in her own world, talking to herself, not really watching. Jayne’s gut shot connects to something in her memory. Her own abdominal injury from the Serenity film. The emergency surgery in rough conditions. The damage that complicated or prevented natural pregnancy. Simon spending two years finding a solution. The line comes out the way memories come out when you’re not watching for them — quietly, to nobody in particular, still not looking up from what she’s fixing:</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">‘Doctor said it wasn’t possible you know. Simon said different.’</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">She’s talking about herself. About Pip. She has no idea Pip is standing in the room. Simon doesn’t hear it — he’s focused on Jayne. Pip hears it. Goes very still in the Simon way. Doesn’t say anything. Doesn’t tell anyone. Thread opens. Paid off later in the season by Jayne sideways: ‘Your father spent two years fixing something the verse broke.’</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Moral Weight</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">This episode is the first time the crew properly encounter the legacy of the Unification War as a living thing rather than history. Mal’s daughter. Zoe’s daughter. The mechanic’s son. They’re retrieving the last physical remnants of the war their parents fought and lost. They don’t know that yet when they take the job. They find out when they get there. The verse doesn’t reward good intentions. Jayne still gets shot.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Serenity Arrival</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Serenity takes damage during the escape from the munitions cache. Engine trouble forces her down ten miles short of Simon and Kaylee’s settlement. The crew take the mule the rest of the way with Jayne bleeding in the back. Pip stays with the ship to fix her.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">When Pip flies Serenity over and lands her in full view of the settlement, the ship is recognised immediately. From his sickbed Jayne: where the hell did you find her. Pip on comms waves back casual as anything — just back from a day’s shopping.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Kaylee sees the ship. Something in her just goes. She runs — not to the crew, not to Pip getting off the ramp, straight past everyone and into the engine room. Baby. Baby. Baby. Simon standing in the doorway watching his wife lose her mind over an engine she hasn’t seen in twenty years.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Then she comes back out. Clocks Pip properly. Clips him round the ear for not being in touch. Then tells him off for using the wrong coolant — Serenity doesn’t like the modern stuff. Pip stands there taking the telling off from his mother who doesn’t know she’s telling off her son. The audience knows. Nobody in the scene does.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.75em; margin-bottom:0.17em; ">The Perceptive Tell — Simon and Kaylee Clock the Crew</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Simon and Kaylee are both highly perceptive people — canon confirmed. Simon reads people the way he reads patients: precise, methodical, analytical. Kaylee reads people through warmth and instinct, the way she reads engines, feeling for what’s underneath.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Across the episode they watch. What they’re seeing: Saff — the silk scarf, the specific quality of stillness that Companions have, the training underneath the tactical vest. Kaylee seeing something in her face she can’t place for a while then places. Cas — the flying, the Hawaiian shirt, and at some point something around her eyes that stops Simon dead. He’s seen those eyes before. On Wash. On Zoe.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Neither Simon nor Kaylee says anything immediately. They’re too careful for that. But across the episode the realisation builds in both of them separately. Two highly perceptive people putting together a picture from mannerisms and details and the specific way children carry their parents without knowing it. The moment it fully lands and they look at each other across the room — no announcement, just understanding simultaneously what is standing in their house — is the emotional payoff of the episode.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Dave reappears. He cracked the second box out of curiosity and found encrypted gibberish he couldn’t read. He’s nervous. Keeping it feels more dangerous than returning it. He gives it back. The crew now have both boxes. The double black box arc moves toward its resolution.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Episode Seven — TBC — SCAFFOLDED</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">One line: A routine data recovery job for Dave turns out to be anything but — and what comes out of the dark explains why the Alliance has been watching them since episode one.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The State of Play</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Weeks after episode six. Jayne is healed. The crew are settled — comfortable silences, shorthand, a ship that feels lived in. This is the most functional they have ever been. That matters because the floor is about to fall out.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Dave makes contact. He has a job. Data recovery from a dead installation on a rim world — an old relay node, dark for twenty years, scrubbed from Alliance records. He needs what’s on it. He doesn’t say why. This is normal for Dave. The pay is modest. They take it anyway.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Act One — The Job</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The installation is exactly what it looks like from the outside. Dead. Abandoned mid-construction. Inside it’s more interesting. Someone was building something serious here — broadcast infrastructure. Long range. The kind of range you’d need to reach every corner of the verse simultaneously.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Pip clocks it immediately. Says nothing yet. Files it away. They pull the data. Clean job. In and out. On the way back to the ship Pip asks Cas a quiet question about the Miranda broadcast — specifically how it was transmitted. Cas doesn’t know why he’s asking. She answers anyway. Pip goes quiet for the rest of the trip back.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Act Two — Dave’s Ship</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Back at Dave’s. Safer to work mobile so they stay on Serenity. Dave comes aboard with his equipment. He and Pip work on the second box together — Dave has been picking at it for weeks, hit a wall, knows Pip is the right person for the next step. They work well together. Dave’s synthetic companion sits in the corner.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The second box gives. Partially. Enough to see the shape of what’s inside but not the content. Encrypted. Layered. Needs a key. Dave spots the first box sitting on the bench. Picks it up. Looks at it. Looks at the second box. Goes very still. The data from the relay installation — buried in the dump, encoded, but there. The key.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Act Three — What’s Inside</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Pip puts the key in. Both boxes open. Dave leans forward and reads. His face does something. He sits back.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The New Wave programme. Not a chemical recreation of the Pax. A fundamental redesign of the delivery mechanism. Two components working together. First: something inert added to water processing infrastructure on rim worlds. Undetectable. Harmless in isolation. The Alliance has been seeding this quietly for years across rim world populations. Nobody knows because nothing happens until the second component activates it. Second: a broadcast frequency transmitted through the relay station network. The frequency interacts with the inert compound already in the body, triggering the body’s own chemical production. The Pax effect is achieved but the source is invisible, the range is controllable, and crucially — it has an off switch. Turn the frequency up: compliance. Turn it higher: something else entirely. The relay stations are not communications infrastructure. They are the transmitters. The network is already built. It is already seeded in rim world populations. It has not been switched on yet. The black boxes are the operating manual for a system that is live and waiting. This is why no Reavers appeared in the New Wave trials the way they did on Miranda. The chemical origin is different. The body produces its own response. The 0.1% outcome is not absent — it is a dial, not an accident. Someone has been quietly rebuilding the relay network from inside the Alliance. Slowly. Without anyone noticing.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Dave plugs the data into his Cortex terminal to cross-reference — needs the processing power, no other option. The moment it touches the Cortex a connection is made somewhere none of them can see. Dave doesn’t know he’s done it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">‘Oh.’ A beat. ‘Oh that’s not good.’ The Mr Universe homage. A man who trades in information understanding for the first time that some information is too heavy to hold.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The Ending</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Later — after Dave has gone, after the ship is quiet — Jayne sits alone in the cargo bay with a wave terminal in his hand. He looks at it for a long time. Then he makes a call. We don’t hear who he calls. We don’t need to.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Post Credits</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The Operator’s screen. A flag. A Cortex access ping — designed to be invisible, wasn’t quite invisible enough. He looks at it. Makes a note. Opens a channel. ‘Find them.’ He closes it. He looks at the flag again. Then at a file he has been building for weeks — photographs, ship registrations, movement patterns. He knows exactly where they are. He has known for a while. He was waiting for the right moment. This is the right moment.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Episode Eight — TBC — SCAFFOLDED</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The Operator stops watching and starts acting. The crew know what’s in the box and the Alliance can’t let them keep breathing. Episode eight is the chase. Every time Serenity tries to go to ground the Alliance are already there or arrive just behind. The ship is damaged from the opening and Pip is keeping her together through sheer stubbornness. Jayne says nothing about where he’s steering them but he is steering them. Two stops. Two near misses. The net is tighter than anyone understood. By the third heading the crew are out of alternatives and Jayne gives coordinates nobody questions. They come down expecting nothing. Mal and Zoe are waiting. Opening: Alliance cruiser finds them. Weapons locked. Cas puts Serenity into a full burn straight at the cruiser — the same logic as episode one, a Firefly at full burn aimed at your hull is not in the manual. She references doing it before. They blink. Serenity gets through but takes damage. Post credits: The Operator. He lost them in the chase. He is not worried. He knows where they are going. He has known for a while.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Episode Nine — THE CAVALRY — SCAFFOLDED</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Anonymous specialists at deep space coordinates. Mal and Zoe. Opening: Mal walks up the ramp like he owns the place — because he does. Looks around the cargo bay. Looks at the crew. Says: Well. You all gonna stand there or are we gonna get to work. Zoe says nothing. She is looking at Cas. The briefing: Saff briefs Mal. She is the captain, she does it cleanly. Mal listens properly. Then he looks at Jayne — long time no see, still working with hired hands out of bars, what was it, Brick and Thick? Mick and Muck. Yeah they didn’t make it. They never do. Back to business. The power struggle: Mal starts moving through the ship the way a man moves through a space he knows. Making calls. Suggesting headings. Saff has to decide whether to let him or push back. The pivot: Mal gives an order to Cas. Cas looks at Saff. The whole episode turns on what Saff does next. Zoe and Cas: runs underneath everything. Zoe watching. The photograph on the console — she sees it, says nothing, files it. The moment: Zoe says Emma. Cas goes still. That’s the whole scene. The plan: by the end they have a heading. The boxes, the relay network, the New Wave programme, River. Mal’s instinct is aggressive. Saff’s call is smarter. Mal recognises his own instinct in her and backs down without admitting it. The ship turns toward episode ten. Post credits: The Operator. The trail is cold. Serenity has dropped off everything — no Cortex ping, no registry, no movement. For the first time all season he doesn’t know where they are. He doesn’t know what they’re doing. He makes a note. Underlines something. But this time he doesn’t pick up the channel. He waits.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Episode Ten — THE GHOST IN THE MACHINE — SCAFFOLDED</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Opening: The test planet. No ships in orbit — the Alliance quarantined it, mined the approach, sent no one. A good sign. They get through. They land. Same eerie picture as Miranda — people at their desks, just stopped. No violence. Just absence. They find the control room, power it up. The research is all there. The workers were fitted with monitors. A concentration of signals in the northwest. They go. Somewhere in there they get into trouble — Reavers stir, something goes wrong. River appears. Not dramatically. Just suddenly she is there and the situation changes. She has been back and forth to this planet for years, keeping the Alliance out, managing the nest. She knows these specific Reavers. She knows what triggers them and what doesn’t. The kids trust her — Pip especially, something in him just knows. Mal has to be talked down from his own instinct. That’s the generational gap paying off. Three generations back to back. The kids do what Mal wouldn’t dare. They get out. They destroy the relay network. The mess room. Beer and pizza. The coat — the brown coat that has been on the ship this whole time, Mal’s ghost carried across nine episodes. He gives it to Saff. ‘Here. You lost yours. Or maybe this one’ll fit you better.’ Post credits: Commissioner Vance. The logs are scrubbed. The New Wave programme never happened. Anyone who knows otherwise doesn’t exist anymore. The crew are the only ones who know. Which is exactly where Mal was after Miranda.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Post credits: Commissioner Vance. The logs are scrubbed. Anyone who knows otherwise doesn’t exist anymore.</p>

<p style="font-size:24px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:2.0em; margin-bottom:0.5em; border-bottom: 2px solid #8B2500; padding-bottom: 0.5em;">The Twenty Year Gap — 2520 to 2542</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">This is our territory. The Dark Horse canon ends around 2520-2521 with the crew intact but fractured. Everything between 2521 and 2542 is our backstory. <p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Cas — The Name</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Our character is Emma Washburne — Zoe and Wash’s daughter, born approximately nine months after the Serenity film, canon from Serenity: Float Out. She goes by Cas.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The name change was entirely practical. She grew up rim world, raised by Zoe the way Zoe raises things — capable, direct, no nonsense. When she struck out on her own and started trying to put crews together, Emma Washburne wasn’t landing her jobs. The name didn’t fit who she was out there. One day someone asked her name and she glanced at something nearby — a crate label, a station sign, a registration plate, something completely mundane — and said Cas. It stuck. She probably doesn’t remember exactly what she saw. That’s how these things happen on the rim.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Zoe calls her Emma. Always. From the moment she was born. It is non-negotiable. That is her daughter’s name and Zoe does not bend on things that matter.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Mal calls her Emma too — at first. He has known she exists since she was born, more or less, from a distance. When she turns up in the vault he calls her Emma because that is who she is to him. But at some point during the season — probably after episode nine, probably after he has watched her operate, watched her make his calls without knowing it — Mal quietly starts calling her Cas. No announcement. No conversation. Just one moment it comes out differently and she clocks it and they both know what it means. He is not calling her by her chosen name out of respect for her autonomy. He is calling her Cas because that is who she earned.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Zoe still calls her Emma. Right through to the end. The name she gave her. The name that carries Wash in it. Two parents. Two names. The same person.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">In episode nine Zoe boards and says Emma. Cas goes very still. The audience does all the work. No explanation needed.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">All he/him references to Cas in previous episode drafts require correction to she/her. The character is otherwise identical — the Hawaiian shirt, the stegosaurus, the flight logs, the photograph of Zoe on the Eleanor’s console. Everything holds. The gender swap makes episode nine more devastating not less — Zoe boards and sees her daughter flying her dead husband’s ship.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The Guild has leverage. Staying means Saff — not yet born, or newly born — grows up in the crosshairs. Leaving is the only way to protect the people she loves most.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Mal lands Serenity. For the first time since he bought her he stops flying. <p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Mal knows where she is. He will not let her be destroyed. Not for anything in the verse. <p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">As Inara’s power grows — eventually to High Priestess of House Madrassa — her ability to keep Serenity hidden grows with it. <p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Saff passed it. He let her train knowing where it led. He helped her walk away not because she wasn’t ready — but because she was.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The teacher would have known too. <p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The teacher leaves after this. Their work is done in ways none of them fully intended. <p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">A prequel has no stakes. A sequel has genuine tension. Nobody has plot armour.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why Jayne as the only original regular</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Always the outsider. Bridges generations without belonging to either.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why Pip is a generalist</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">He doesn’t fall in love with Fireflies. He falls in love with this one. That distinction is the difference between a nice moment and a devastating one.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why Saff makes the call to save Cas</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Pip is the pragmatist. Saff is the conscience. Mal’s daughter making Mal’s choice without knowing it.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why Cas is the bounty</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Dramatic irony. Audience understands the full picture before Cas does. His reaction defines his character in one beat.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why the Operator watches instead of acts</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">A villain who sends troops is a logistical problem. A villain who watches is a philosophical one.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why three separate crew fates for Cas</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Rex runs, Decker dies, Lena is captured. Three different wounds. Together they give Cas’s self-assessment specific weight rather than general guilt.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why Decker clicks the safety</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">One sound. No explanation needed. Creates the echo when he dies and Jayne appears.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why Kaylee’s backstory is never stated directly</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Two moments planted episodes apart. Paid off by Jayne sideways. The audience does the work. The reward is enormous.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why episode one ends on the ship in the black</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The escape is not the ending. The ship flying again is the ending. Roll credits on that image.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why episode two opens without dialogue</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The kids have just survived something enormous. Guards completely down. The score does the work. Three discoveries in three parts of the ship simultaneously.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why Jayne’s carving and not a speech</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Because Jayne doesn’t make speeches. Thirty years of history lives in those crude letters better than any words he could say.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why the grav boot and not the compression coil</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The compression coil is already a three-episode running joke in the original. Fresh ground. Same DNA.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why the Serenity name reveal belongs to the Shepherds</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The name should not be announced. It should be recognised. An older Shepherd who knew the ship says it quietly. The kids don’t understand. Jayne does. The audience does. That gap is everything.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why the broker is called Dave</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Because it’s funny. Not ha-ha funny. Just completely unexpected and therefore completely right. No explanation. No fanfare. Just Dave. The joke is that nobody ever comments on it.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why the double black box and not a single one</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">The first box is the mystery. The second box is the key. Neither of them knows it for four episodes. When Pip realises the connection the payoff is enormous because the audience has been watching it approach since episode three. Dave holding the key without knowing it is the Mr Universe homage landing in the right place — not in the setup but in the weight of realisation.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:1.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Why Dave cracks the second box and not the crew</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.25em; margin-bottom:0.42em; ">Dave is the information broker. Of course he cracked it out of curiosity. Where Mr Universe loved data for its own sake, Dave found something he couldn’t read and got frightened. That distinction — curiosity becoming fear rather than excitement — keeps him his own character while the homage still lands.</p>
