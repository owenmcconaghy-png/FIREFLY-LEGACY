## One — The Wave


---

*Serenity — The Mess Room — Morning*

Four weeks out from the settlement and the ship has found her rhythm.

Not the rhythm of four strangers navigating a situation. The other kind.
The kind that doesn't need to announce itself.

JAYNE is at the table with coffee and no hat which means he is not
expecting trouble today and has decided to be comfortable about it.

CAS has the nav charts out. Not because anything needs plotting. Just
because she thinks better with something to look at.

PIP is in the engine room. The sound of him talking to something that
cannot talk back has become part of the ship's ambient noise.

SAFF is reading something on her tablet with the focused expression of a
woman who is pretending to read and is actually thinking.

The Cortex chimes.

Personal wave. For Saff.

She looks at the identifier. Looks at Jayne.

> **SAFF** It's Dave.

Jayne picks up his coffee.

> **JAYNE** Course it is.

She opens the channel.

DAVE on the screen. Fifties. The specific expression of a man who has
been sitting with something for a while and has decided what to do about
it.

> **DAVE** Got a job. If you want it.
>
> **SAFF** What kind.
>
> **DAVE** Data recovery. Dead installation on a rim world. Been dark
> twenty years. Someone wants what's on it.
>
> **SAFF** Someone being you.
>
> **DAVE** Someone being a client who prefers not to be named. Which in
> my experience means they're either embarrassed or dangerous and in
> this case I believe it's the former.

A beat.

> **SAFF** Pay.
>
> **DAVE** Modest.

Saff looks at Jayne. Jayne looks at his coffee.

> **JAYNE** We need the coin.

Saff looks back at the screen.

> **SAFF** Send us the coordinates.
>
> **DAVE** Already did.

He closes the channel.

Cas is already at the nav chart.

Pip's voice from the engine room, without being asked:

> **PIP** *(over ship-wide)* She's ready when you are.

Nobody asked how he knew.

Nobody needed to.

## Two — The Installation


---

*Rim World — Dead Relay Node — Day*

The installation sits in a valley between two ridges that have no name
on any current chart. The kind of place you find when you already know
where you're looking.

From the outside it is exactly what it looks like. Dead. The kind of
dead that happened slowly and was never reversed.

Serenity sets down half a mile out. The crew come the rest of the way on
foot.

JAYNE clocks the structure first. Says nothing. Just looks at it the way
he looks at buildings he is about to enter without permission.

SAFF reads the perimeter. No active sensors. No power signature. No
guards.

> **SAFF** Twenty years dark.
>
> **JAYNE** Alliance doesn't just abandon installations.
>
> **SAFF** This one they did.

Jayne looks at her.

> **JAYNE** That bother you.

A beat.

> **SAFF** Little bit.

They go in anyway.

--- — —


---

*Dead Relay Node — Interior — Continuous*

Inside it is cold and dark and smells like a place that was sealed and
has been breathing the same air ever since.

CAS has the light. SAFF has the schematics Dave sent. They move through
corridors that were built to Alliance specification and have not
deviated from it since.

PIP is quiet.

This is unusual. Pip is never quiet in a building. He reads
infrastructure the way Cas reads a cockpit — instinctively,
continuously, out loud if nobody stops him.

He is not talking.

He is looking.

At the conduit runs. At the relay housings along the walls. At the scale
of the antenna array visible through a viewport at the far end of the
main corridor.

Saff clocks him.

> **SAFF** Pip.
>
> **PIP** I'm fine.
>
> **SAFF** Didn't ask.

A beat.

> **PIP** This is big infrastructure. For a relay node.
>
> **SAFF** How big.
>
> **PIP** Verse-wide big. If it was finished.

Nobody says anything to that.

They keep moving.

--- — —


---

*Dead Relay Node — Server Room — Continuous*

The data is where Dave said it would be. The server housing cold and
dark and waiting.

Pip gets it open in four minutes. Runs a line. Watches the transfer
complete.

Disconnects. Closes the housing.

> **PIP** Got it.

They go.

Behind them the installation sits in its valley and waits for the next
thing to happen to it.

It has waited before.

## Three — Dave's Ship


---

*Serenity — The Mess Room — After The Job*

Pip has his boots off. Cas has her feet up. Saff is on her second
coffee.

The particular quiet of a crew back on a ship after nothing went wrong.

> **JAYNE** Smooth.

Nobody says anything.

> **JAYNE** I\${APOS}m just saying. That went smooth.

The Cortex chimes.

Everyone looks at Jayne.

> **JAYNE** That's not on me.

DAVE on the screen. The specific expression of a man who has found
something and is not entirely comfortable about it.

> **DAVE** Come pick me up. I have something to show you.

Saff looks at the others.

> **SAFF** We're on our way.

--- — —


---

*Serenity — The Cargo Bay — Later*

The mule brings Dave back up the ramp.

He comes aboard with a case of equipment and the second box.

He sets the box on the workbench.

> **DAVE** I cracked the outer shell. Six weeks. What I found inside
> looked like gibberish.

A beat.

> **DAVE** Encrypted gibberish. Which is different.
>
> **PIP** Different how.
>
> **DAVE** Gibberish means nothing. Encrypted gibberish means something
> someone very much didn't want read.

He looks at the box.

> **DAVE** Keeping it feels more dangerous than returning it.

He slides it across the bench toward Pip.

Pip looks at it. Looks at Saff. Looks back at the box.

His synthetic companion sits in the corner of the cargo bay. Unhurried.
Present. A little outside of everything.

Jayne looks at her.

Looks away.

Says nothing.

> **SAFF** You said you had something to show us.
>
> **DAVE** I do.

He opens his case. Sets up at the workbench with the efficiency of a man
who knows exactly what he needs.

> **DAVE** I can't get past the encryption alone. But I think together
> we can.

He looks at Pip.

> **DAVE** Your kind of problem.

Pip pulls up a stool.

> **PIP** Show me what you've done.

--- — —


---

*Serenity — The Cargo Bay — Later*

Two hours in.

Dave works the encryption layer on the second box. Pip works the
physical housing. They don't talk much. What they say is brief and
incomprehensible to anyone not already inside the problem.

The others have settled into the cargo bay the way people settle into
spaces where work is happening that they can't help with. Saff on the
stairs. Cas with a nav chart. Jayne in his corner with something to eat.

Dave gets through another layer.

Pip opens the housing. Looks at the architecture inside.

Frowns.

> **PIP** There's a physical lock I can't shift. Built into the hardware
> itself.
>
> **DAVE** Same on my end. The encryption needs an external key.
> Something built specifically for this box.

Pip turns the second box over in his hands.

Then he looks at the first box sitting where he left it on the bench.

He picks it up. Turns it over.

Sets both boxes side by side.

Dave looks at them.

Something happens in his face.

> **DAVE** Where did you get that one.
>
> **PIP** Alliance vault. Persephone. First job we ever ran together.
>
> **DAVE** They were built together.

Pip looks at both boxes.

Looks at Dave.

Looks back.

> **PIP** One unlocks the other.
>
> **DAVE** Or the second is the key to the first. Depends which way you
> look at it.

A long beat.

Pip reaches for the first box.

Gets to work.

The cargo bay is very quiet.

Saff is watching from the stairs.

She doesn't know what she's watching yet.

But she knows it's something.

--- — —


---

*Serenity — The Cargo Bay — Later*

Pip sits back.

Looks at the boxes.

> **PIP** Oh.

Quiet. To nobody.

He sits there for a moment.

Then he stands up.

> **PIP** Saff.

Saff comes down the stairs.

Cas appears in the doorway. Jayne doesn\${APOS}t move but he is
listening.

> **PIP** Both of you. Come and look at this.

## Four — What's Inside


---

*Serenity — The Cargo Bay — Later*

Pip sits back.

Looks at the boxes.

> **PIP** Oh.

Quiet. To nobody.

He sits there for a moment.

Then he stands up.

> **PIP** Saff.

Saff comes down the stairs.

Cas appears in the doorway. Jayne doesn't move but he is listening.

> **PIP** Both of you. Come and look at this.

They come.

The boxes are open. Data on the screen. Files. Schematics. Research
logs. A lot of it.

Dave is reading.

He has been reading since the moment they opened and he has not said
anything.

The crew look at the screens. Look at Dave.

Dave keeps reading.

Then he stops.

Sits back.

Looks at the boxes.

Looks at his equipment.

> **DAVE** I need to take these back with me.
>
> **SAFF** Why.
>
> **DAVE** Because I need more processing power than I have here and I
> need to understand what I'm looking at before I say anything about it.

Saff looks at him.

> **SAFF** What did you see.

Dave looks at the screen.

At something specific.

Back at Saff.

> **DAVE** Let me read it properly first.

A beat.

Saff looks at Jayne. Jayne looks at Dave.

Dave is already packing up his equipment.

> **SAFF** How long.
>
> **DAVE** Day. Maybe two.

He picks up the boxes.

Looks at Pip.

> **DAVE** Good work.

Pip says nothing.

Dave goes down the ramp. His synthetic companion follows without being
called.

The crew watch him go.

The cargo bay is very quiet.

> **CAS** That's not good.
>
> **JAYNE** No.

He picks up what he was eating.

Looks at it.

Puts it down.

## Five — Two Days


---

*Serenity — Various — Two Days*

The ship moves. No particular heading. Just keeping the black between
them and anywhere specific.

Day one is quiet.

Day two is quieter.

Pip is in the engine room. Cas is in the cockpit. Saff is doing what
Saff does when she is waiting for something — moving through the ship
slowly, touching things, thinking.

Jayne is at the table with coffee and the specific stillness of a man
who is not worried and would like that to be noted.

Nobody notes it.

--- — —


---

*Dave's Location — Day One*

Dave's system runs through the night.

The boxes sit open on his desk. His equipment processes. His synthetic
companion sits in her chair and does not ask questions because she never
asks questions and tonight that is exactly what Dave needs.

By morning he has enough to know this is going to take a long time.

--- — —


---

*Dave's Location — Day Two*

He looks at the data.

Looks at his closed system.

> **DAVE** *(to himself)* At this rate my grandchildren will be the
> first to read it.

He opens the Cortex connection.

Puts his feet up on the console.

Picks up his ramen.

The analysis accelerates.

His synthetic companion sits in her chair. The room hums. Outside the
black does what the black does.

Dave eats his ramen.

--- — —


---

*Dave's Location — Six Hours Later*

The alarm is not loud.

It doesn't need to be.

Dave's feet come off the console.

The ramen is cold. He doesn\${APOS}t notice. He is looking at the
screen.

Someone is trying to breach his system.

Not a random probe. Not automated. Something deliberate. Something that
knows exactly where to look.

Dave looks at the Cortex connection.

Looks at the screen.

Closes the connection.

Sits very still.

His synthetic companion looks at him from her chair.

Dave looks at the analysis results on his screen.

At what six hours of Cortex processing has returned.

He reads.

For a long time he reads.

Then he picks up the wave terminal.

--- — —


---

*Serenity — The Cockpit — Day Two — Later*

The Cortex chimes.

CAS opens the channel.

DAVE on the screen. The specific expression of a man who has been
sitting with something for two days and has decided he cannot sit with
it alone any longer.

> **DAVE** Come back. Now.

Cas looks at the screen for a moment.

> **CAS** On our way.

--- — —

## Six — What Dave Found


---

*Dave's Location — Later*

The crew arrive to find Dave at his desk with the particular stillness
of a man who has not moved in several hours and is not sure he wants to.

His synthetic companion is in her chair. The screens are full of data.
The ramen bowl is still there, cold, forgotten.

Dave looks at the crew.

At Saff. At Jayne. At Pip. At Cas.

He looks at Pip for a moment longer.

> **DAVE** Sit down.

Nobody sits down.

> **DAVE** Sit down.

They sit down.

Dave turns to the screens.

> **DAVE** You know what Miranda was.

Not a question.

> **DAVE** You know what the Pax did. What it was supposed to do. What
> it actually did.

A beat.

> **DAVE** After Miranda went public the Alliance buried it. The
> programme. The research. The people who knew about it. All of it.
>
> **DAVE** But some of them kept working.

He pulls up a file on the screen. Research logs. Dates. A small world on
the outer rim that doesn't appear on any current chart.

> **DAVE** Not a reworked formula. A completely different approach. Two
> components working together.

A beat.

> **DAVE** First — something inert. Added quietly to rim world water
> processing for years. Undetectable. Harmless on its own. Nobody knows
> because nothing happens until the second component activates it.

He looks at the crew.

> **DAVE** Second — a broadcast frequency. Transmitted through the
> relay network. The frequency interacts with what's already in the
> body. Triggers the body's own chemical production. No air processors.
> No mass deployment. Just a signal.

The room is very quiet.

> **DAVE** And it has an off switch. Turn the frequency up —
> compliance. Turn it higher —

He doesn\${APOS}t finish that sentence.

Nobody needs him to.

A long beat.

> **SAFF** They were going to broadcast it everywhere.
>
> **DAVE** Yes.
>
> **SAFF** If they hadn't caught the delay —
>
> **DAVE** They would have delivered a three month delay to every living
> person in the verse. And then.

He doesn\${APOS}t finish the sentence.

He doesn\${APOS}t need to.

Jayne is looking at the screen.

At the map. The nodes. The ones that are lit.

He stands up.

> **JAYNE** Turn it off.

Dave looks at him.

> **JAYNE** Turn it off Dave.
>
> **DAVE** Jayne —
>
> **JAYNE** These kids don\${APOS}t need this.

A beat.

> **JAYNE** They don\${APOS}t need to be carrying this.

The crew look at Jayne.

He is not looking at any of them.

Saff looks at the screen. At the map. At the lit nodes.

Looks at Jayne.

> **SAFF** We're already in it. We pulled that data. We opened those
> boxes.

A beat.

> **SAFF** We need to know.

Jayne looks at her.

At Mal's daughter.

A long moment.

Then he sits back down.

> **JAYNE** Show them.

Dave turns back to the screen.

He tells them.

All of it.

Two components. An inert compound seeded into rim world water processing
for years without anyone knowing. A broadcast frequency through the
relay network that activates it in the body. No Miranda-style accident.
A dial. Controllable. Precise. The network already built. The
populations already seeded.

It just hasn't been switched on yet.

The project buried. The nodes abandoned in place. Too dangerous to
dismantle.

And someone powering them back up.

The crew listen.

Nobody interrupts.

Nobody asks questions until he stops talking and the room settles into
the specific silence of people who have just been handed something they
cannot put down.

> **CAS** The installation we pulled the data from.
>
> **DAVE** One of the original nodes. Whoever is doing this found the
> research the same way you did.
>
> **PIP** They're not starting from scratch.
>
> **DAVE** No.

A beat.

> **SAFF** What do we do with this.

Dave looks at the boxes.

Then he pulls up another file.

And another.

And another.

Names. Faces. Records. The original crew of Serenity. What they did.
What Miranda cost them. What they gave the verse that the verse never
fully gave back.

The crew look at the screen.

At the faces.

> **DAVE** Your parents built the world that makes this worth stopping.

A long silence.

Saff looks at Jayne.

Cas looks at Jayne.

Pip looks at Jayne.

Jayne is looking at the screen.

At the faces.

He says nothing.

--- — —

Dave closes the files.

Looks at the data. At what six hours of processing returned. At what it
means.

He sits back.

> **DAVE** Oh.

Quiet.

He looks at the Cortex terminal.

> **DAVE** Oh that's not good.

The crew look at him.

> **SAFF** What.
>
> **DAVE** The breach on my system. Earlier.

A beat.

> **DAVE** They weren't trying to get in.

He looks at the terminal.

> **DAVE** They already were.

The room is very still.

Dave looks at the crew.

> **DAVE** They know what you opened. They know where you are.

Silence.

Then Jayne stands up.

> **JAYNE** We need to go.

Nobody argues.

## Seven — The Call


---

*Serenity — The Cargo Bay — Later*

The ship is moving.

No heading. Just away from where they were.

The cargo bay is quiet. The kind of quiet that follows a room full of
information nobody asked for.

Cas is in the cockpit. Saff is on the stairs. Pip is in the engine room
because that is where Pip goes when he needs to think.

Jayne is in his corner.

Sitting.

Not eating. Not sleeping. Just sitting with his hands on the table and
the specific stillness of a man working something out.

Saff watches him from the stairs.

He doesn't look up.

--- — —


---

*Serenity — The Cargo Bay — Later Still*

The ship hums.

Pip's voice from the engine room. Quiet. To nobody.

Cas on the comms giving coordinates to herself because there are no
coordinates. Just the black.

Saff has gone to her bunk.

Jayne is still at the table.

He looks at the wave terminal in his hand.

Looks at it for a long time.

Then he opens a channel.

Types a call sign that nobody on this ship knows he has.

Waits.

The channel connects.

He doesn't say much. He never does. Just enough. The coordinates. The
situation. What's in the boxes.

A pause on the other end.

Then a voice. Two words. That\${APOS}s all.

Jayne closes the channel.

Sets the terminal down.

Looks at the ceiling of the cargo bay.

The same ceiling.

He closes his eyes.
