<p style="color:#555555; font-size:16px; font-weight:bold; text-align:center; margin-top:1.5em; margin-bottom:1.5em; ">One — Rattle Rock Station</p>

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">Rattle Rock Station — Docking Bay — Day</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Rattle Rock Station is exactly what it sounds like.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">It clings to the surface of Huang Xing Six’s third moon the way things cling to surfaces out here — not because the location is good but because someone decided to stop here once and then couldn’t afford to leave. The docking bay is carved directly into the rock. Low ceiling. Dim lighting. The smell of recycled air and machine oil and the particular staleness of a place that has been breathing the same atmosphere for twenty years without complaining about it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The ship settles into her berth with a sound that is half mechanical and half something else — the specific sigh of a vessel that has been flying for four days with a grav boot in declining health and is quietly relieved to stop.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The rattle subsides.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">For a moment everything is still.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Then PIP is under the ship before the landing struts have fully extended, diagnostic tool out, already talking to the grav boot with the focused murmuring of a man conducting a very important conversation with something that cannot talk back.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP  (murmuring to the engine)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">I know. I know. We’re here. You did well.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">SAFF comes down the ramp and looks at his boots sticking out from under the hull.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">How long does she need.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP  (from under the ship)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">She’s fine now we’re not moving. She just needs the part.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Can she fly if we need to leave fast.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">A pause. The kind that answers the question before the words do.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Define fast.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Fast like we’re being shot at.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Probably. Mostly. Don’t push her past point eight and avoid anything that requires a sudden change of vector.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">That’s nearly everything.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Then avoid nearly everything.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">CAS appears at the top of the ramp. He looks around the docking bay with the professional assessment of a pilot clocking exits. Three of them. Two serviceable. One that probably hasn’t opened since the war.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">CAS</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Cheerful place.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE  (already walking toward the far exit)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">It’s got a bar.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Jayne. We’re not here for the bar.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Dave’s office is above the bar. I wasn’t talking about the bar.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">A beat.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE  (still walking)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">The bar’s also good though.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:16px; font-weight:bold; text-align:center; margin-top:1.5em; margin-bottom:1.5em; ">Two — Dave</p>

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">Rattle Rock Station — Above The Bar — Continuous</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The office is one flight up from the bar and it is a room that takes some time to fully take in.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Not because it is large — it isn’t, the ceiling is low and the walls are raw rock and the whole space has the feel of something that was not so much built as accumulated. But the screens. There are screens on every surface that can hold one, arranged in overlapping arrays that cover the walls from floor to ceiling, each showing a different feed from a different source. News. Traffic patterns. Wave frequencies. Markets. Encrypted channels that flicker with data too fast to read. The combined light of them fills the room with a blue-white glow that makes everyone in it look like they’re underwater.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">In the middle of all of it, at a desk that is less a desk and more a flat surface upon which several decades of hardware have been arranged into a working relationship, sits DAVE.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Dave is fifties. Medium height. The kind of face that you look at and immediately forget, which is probably useful in his line of work. He is wearing a knitted sweater that has seen better decades and drinking something hot from a mug that says BEST DAD in large cheerful letters. He does not look up when they come in.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He is reading something.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">In the corner of the room, in a chair that has been placed there with some care — angled toward the screens, toward the light, toward Dave — sits VERA.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She is still. Not the stillness of someone waiting. The stillness of someone who is simply present. Her hands are in her lap. She is looking at nothing in particular with the patient quality of a person who has nowhere else to be and has made her peace with that.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She is not a person.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She is very well made. She has been in that chair for an unknown amount of time because Dave likes her there.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">JAYNE walks in, clocks the screens, clocks Vera in the corner, and goes completely still.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">It lasts one second. Maybe two.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Then:</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE  (to the room, to nobody, to the memory)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Man I knew had one of these.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Dave looks up.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Jayne.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Dave.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">CAS looks at SAFF. Saff looks at PIP. Pip is looking at the screens with the specific expression of someone trying very hard not to touch anything.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE  (still looking at Vera)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Beautiful ceremony. She wrote her own vows.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Dave considers this for a moment.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">She did. I wrote them for her but she delivered them herself. Which counts.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He says this with complete sincerity. Jayne nods once. The subject is closed. They have understood each other perfectly.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Dave looks at the three behind Jayne.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">These your crew?</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">More or less.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Which is it.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Still working that out.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Dave nods. He stands up and extends a hand to Saff, who is closest, with the manner of a man who has decided that normal social conventions are worth maintaining even in an office carved into a moon.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Dave.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Saff.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She shakes his hand. Her expression does not flicker. It is only later, when she replays the introduction in her head, that she will have a question about it. She files it away for now.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE  (to Cas, who shakes it)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Cas.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE  (to Pip, who is still looking at the screens)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Pip.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Sorry. Yes. Sorry. Are those — are those all live feeds?</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Most of them.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">How many sources?</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Currently? About four hundred.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Pip looks at the screens. Looks at Dave. Looks back at the screens.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Right. Yes. Hello. I’m Pip.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Dave almost smiles. He sits back down. Gets straight to it in the manner of a man for whom time is the scarcest resource.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Series Three grav boot mount. Original manufacture. You’re lucky — I know a parts dealer on Persephone who has one. Had it for years, waiting for someone to need it. The price is going to hurt.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">How much.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Dave tells her.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The room is quiet for a moment.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">That’s —</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF  (before Pip finishes)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Fine.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">That’s really quite a lot of —</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Pip.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Fine. Yes. Fine.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF  (to Dave)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">We don’t have that.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">I know. That’s why I have a job for you first.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He pulls up something on the desk surface. A schematic. A location. A facility on the surface of a moon three systems out — rim world, small, the kind of place that doesn’t appear on most charts because it was never meant to appear on most charts.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Alliance research outpost. Minimal staff. Light security by Alliance standards which still means don’t be stupid about it. There’s an item in their primary storage that a client of mine wants very much.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">CAS</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">What kind of item.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">The valuable kind.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">CAS</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">That’s not an answer.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE  (not unkindly)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">It’s the answer you’re getting.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Cas looks at Jayne. Jayne shrugs with the specific economy of a man who has done business this way for forty years and made his peace with it.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">There’s an Abbey on the same moon. Shepherd community. They’ve been watching that outpost for years — they know the patrol schedules, the shift changes, the two guards who take an extra twenty minutes on their evening break because one of them is teaching the other to play cards. You want that information, you talk to the Shepherds first.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">You’re sending us to an Abbey for a robbery.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">I’m sending you to an Abbey for intelligence. What you do with it is your business.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Saff looks at him for a moment.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">And the part.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">DAVE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">I’ll have it sourced and waiting by the time you get back. You bring me what I need, you get the part. Clean transaction.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The four of them look at each other. The silent conversation that crews have when they’ve been together long enough to conduct one. Pip’s eyes go from Saff to the screens and back. Cas’s eyes go to the schematic on the desk. Jayne’s eyes don’t move.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Saff makes a decision.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Send us the coordinates.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:16px; font-weight:bold; text-align:center; margin-top:1.5em; margin-bottom:1.5em; ">Three — En Route</p>

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">The Ship — Mess Room — In Transit</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The schematic is spread across the mess table. Real paper — Dave’s preference, apparently, for anything he considers sensitive. The kind of document that doesn’t leave a data trail.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">CAS has been studying it for twenty minutes. He has the focused stillness of someone who is not reading a document so much as memorising it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">SAFF is at the far end of the table with a mug of coffee going cold beside her. She is not looking at the schematic. She is looking at the space above it — the middle distance that means she is running through something in her head, testing it, finding its edges.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">PIP comes in from the direction of the engine room. He is wiping his hands on a cloth that was clean this morning. He sits down and looks at the schematic upside down from his end of the table.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">She’s holding. For now.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">How long.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Long enough. Probably. Don’t ask me to be more specific than probably.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Saff nods. Probably is what they have.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">JAYNE appears in the doorway with a plate of something that might be food. He sits in his corner chair without ceremony and starts eating.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">CAS</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">The facility has two entry points. Front entrance — guarded, keycard access, not worth the trouble. Service entrance at the east side — keycard but older model, the kind that’s been on the same code for two years because nobody updated it.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">How do you know they haven’t updated it.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">CAS</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Because Dave knew they hadn’t. Which means he’s had eyes on this place for a while.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">A beat.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Which means he knew he was going to need someone to do this job before we walked through his door.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Nobody says anything. This is obvious once said and mildly unsettling once obvious.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE  (eating)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Dave always has someone for a job. That’s how Dave works.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Were we specifically someone or just someone.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Does it change anything.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Saff considers this.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF  (after a moment)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">No.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE  (still eating)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Then stop asking.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Pip looks at the schematic. Turns it around so it’s the right way up for him. Studies the layout with the same methodical attention he gives the engine.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Primary storage is here. Centre of the building. No windows. Two internal doors between us and it once we’re through the service entrance.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">CAS</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">The Shepherds will know the patrol timing. If Dave’s right about the card game we’ve got a twenty minute window in the evening rotation.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">We talk to the Shepherds first. We don’t move until we know the window is real.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">CAS</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Agreed.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She picks up her coffee. Finds it cold. Drinks it anyway.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Jayne. The Shepherds. Any history I should know about.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">With me?</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">With you.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Jayne sets his fork down. Picks it back up. Sets it down again.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Man I knew had some. With the Shepherds. Not me specifically.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">What kind of history.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">The complicated kind. Old now. They’ll know the ship though.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He picks up his fork again. The subject is, apparently, closed.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Saff files it away. She is getting good at filing things Jayne says away.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:16px; font-weight:bold; text-align:center; margin-top:1.5em; margin-bottom:1.5em; ">Four — The Abbey</p>

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">Constance Moon — The Abbey of the Penitent Road — Day</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The moon is green. That is the first thing. After weeks of rock and station and the brown-grey palette of frontier infrastructure the green is almost aggressive — low hills covered in something that is almost grass, a sky the colour of pale jade, the particular quality of light that comes through clouds that are never quite gone and never quite there.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The Abbey sits on a rise above a small valley. Stone walls. Old ones — the kind that were built to last and have been doing exactly that for long enough that the distinction between the wall and the hill beneath it has become uncertain. A bell tower. A garden. And at the gate, watching the ship land in the flat ground below with the expression of someone who has been watching things land here for a very long time, a SHEPHERD.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She is old. Not ancient, not frail, but the specific old of someone who has spent a great many years outdoors in all weathers doing things that required both patience and physical capability. Her habit is dark and practical. Her eyes are the kind that don’t miss much and have long since stopped being polite about it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The crew come up the path from the landing ground. Jayne first, which is unusual. He walks with his hands visible and his shoulders at a particular angle that Saff has started to recognise as his version of non-threatening.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The Shepherd watches them come.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She watches Jayne.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Then she looks past him.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">At the ship.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She looks at the ship the way you look at something you recognise from a long time ago and weren’t sure you’d see again. Her expression doesn’t change dramatically. Just something in the set of it. Something settling.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Very quietly. Almost to herself.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SHEPHERD</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Serenity.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Not an exclamation. Not a revelation. Just a name.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">CAS hears it. Looks at the ship. Looks at the Shepherd. The Shepherd has already moved on, walking toward them with her hands behind her back.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">SAFF heard it. She looks at Cas. She gives her the very small headshake that means not now.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">PIP heard it. He looks at his own hands. Then at the ship. Then back ahead.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">JAYNE heard it. He doesn’t look at the Shepherd. He looks at the ship for just a second — something crossing his face that doesn’t have a name in any language spoken on any moon in the Verse — and then he looks forward and keeps walking.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SHEPHERD  (reaching them, looking at Jayne)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">You’ll want Brother Aldric. He’s been watching that outpost since before it was an outpost.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">We’re grateful for any help the Abbey can offer.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The Shepherd looks at him for a long moment.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SHEPHERD  (not a question)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">You’ve been here before.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Not me personally.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SHEPHERD  (looking at the ship again, then back)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">No. Not you.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She turns and walks toward the Abbey gate.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SHEPHERD</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Come in. We’ll feed you. Brother Aldric talks better on a full stomach. Most people do.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">The Abbey — The Refectory — Later</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">BROTHER ALDRIC is seventies and has the build of someone who spent the first half of his life doing something considerably more physical than prayer. His hands are large and his nose has been broken at least twice and he eats with the focus of a man who treats meals as a practical matter rather than a social one.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He has been talking for twenty minutes. He has not once checked whether they are keeping up. They are keeping up.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">ALDRIC</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Evening patrol starts at six. Two guards. They do the perimeter first — twenty minutes if they’re prompt, thirty if Sergeant Hollis has been drinking, which on a Seconday he usually has. Then they split. One goes internal. One stays on the east side.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">CAS</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">The east side guard. Is that consistent?</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">ALDRIC</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">For three years. The internal guard is the one who plays cards. Private Keel. He teaches a new one every rotation — currently it’s the young woman on comms who is apparently very bad at it and hasn’t worked that out yet. They’re usually in the break room by seven fifteen. Back on post by seven forty at the latest.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Cas looks at Saff. Twenty-five minutes. More than enough.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">The storage room. Internal security.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">ALDRIC</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Keypad. Six digit. Changed monthly but they use the same rotation — the last three digits are always the installation date of the outpost. The first three…</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He reaches into his habit and produces a folded piece of paper. Sets it on the table. Pip picks it up and reads it. His expression goes very still in the Simon way.</p>
