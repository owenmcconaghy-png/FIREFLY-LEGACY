*In which Saff Reynolds goes to find her mother.*

*One — Setting Course*

**The Ship — The Mess Room — Morning**
CAS is already there when SAFF comes in.
She has the same coffee, the same chair, the same hands wrapped around the mug. But she watches her come in differently than she watched her come in yesterday. Something about the way she moves through the doorway. More purpose in it than usual, which for Saff Reynolds is saying something.
She pours coffee. Sits down. Puts both hands flat on the table.
Doesn’t drink.
SAFF
I need a heading.
Cas looks at her over her mug.
CAS
Where.
She tells her. A planet. A location within it. Specific enough that she knows she’s had this ready for a while.
CAS
How long.
SAFF
Three days. Maybe four if she needs it easy.
She nods. Sets her mug down. Gets up.
CAS
I’ll set it now.
He doesn’t ask why. He doesn’t ask what’s there. He just goes.
Saff watches her go. Picks up her coffee. Drinks.
The ship hums. Somewhere forward Cas is already at the helm, already finding the heading, already giving Serenity something to point at.
The mess room is quiet.
Then JAYNE walks in.
He has a plate and he has the hat and he has the specific expression of a man whose morning routine has been entirely satisfactory up to this point. He sits in his corner. Loads his fork.
Looks at the heading on the small nav display set into the mess room wall that nobody ever looks at because nobody needs to.
He looks at it for a moment.
Sets his fork down.
JAYNE
What in the gorramn hell are we doing headed there.
Saff wraps both hands around her mug.
SAFF
Guild planet.
JAYNE
I know it’s a Guild planet. Why are we going to a Guild planet.
SAFF
I need to find someone.
JAYNE
On a Guild planet.
SAFF
On a Guild planet.
Jayne picks up his fork. Sets it down again. The plate gets pushed aside. This is a conversation that requires both hands free.
JAYNE
Who.
A beat.
SAFF
My mother.
The mess room is very quiet.
Jayne looks at her. At the silk scarf around her neck. At the specific quality of stillness she has that he has been recognising for a while without being able to name it.
He names it now.
JAYNE  (quietly, not quite a question)
Inara Serra is on a Guild planet.
SAFF
High Priestess of House Madrassa.
Jayne is very still.
JAYNE  (very quietly)
You’re a stupid girl.
SAFF
I’ve been called worse.
JAYNE
The Guild is not a place you walk into. You understand that. People who walk into the Guild without an invitation walk out changed or they don’t walk out. They trade in leverage the way —
He stops himself. Something crossing his face.
JAYNE
They know about Mal. They know about all of it. They’ve got Alliance connections going back thirty years and they have been sitting on everything they know about that ship and everyone who ever flew on her and if you walk in there and announce yourself as Mal Reynolds’ daughter —
SAFF
I know.
JAYNE
Do you.
SAFF  (quietly)
I know how to walk out of one.
She doesn’t elaborate. Doesn’t explain.
Jayne looks at her for a long moment.
JAYNE
I want no part of this.
SAFF
Nobody needs a part of it.
She stands up. Picks up her mug. Looks at him.
SAFF
I’m taking the shuttle. I’ll be back.
She walks out.
Jayne sits in his corner with his pushed-aside plate and his cold coffee and the nav display on the wall showing a heading he doesn’t like.
He sits there for a long time.
Then he picks up his fork.
Eats.
Doesn’t taste it.

*Two — Sihnon*

**Deep Space — Exterior — Approach to Sihnon**
The planet comes up on sensors the way Core worlds always come up. Almost too bright. Too deliberate. The kind of world that has been shaped and tended by centuries of human intention until it glows.
Sihnon.
Blue and white and vast. Nothing like the brown-grey rim worlds they have been living in. Nothing like rock and dust and stations that smell of machine oil. This planet is polished. This planet knows it.
**The Ship — The Cockpit — Same Time**
CAS sits at the helm looking at Sihnon through the cockpit glass. PIP leans in the doorway behind her.
Neither of them says anything for a moment.
PIP
Bit different.
CAS
Bit.
They look at it a while longer.
PIP  (to the planet, not really asking)
Wonder what she wants with a Core world.
A beat.
CAS
That’s her business.
Pip nods. Puts the piece of hardware back in his pocket.
CAS
Shuttle’s ready.
**The Ship — Shuttle Dock — Minutes Later**
SAFF moves through the hatch with the particular economy of someone who has done the thing they’ve been preparing to do and is now simply doing it.
No ceremony. No hesitation.
She is wearing the silk scarf.
She always wears the silk scarf. But today it is arranged differently — not loose over the tactical vest but properly. The way it was meant to be worn. The way someone taught her to wear it before she decided she didn’t want to be taught.
The hatch closes behind her.
The shuttle undocks.
**Inara’s Shuttle — Cockpit — Continuous**
Saff settles into the cockpit. The shuttle is small. Intimate. The silk hangings visible through the open hatch behind her.
She puts her hands on the controls.
Sihnon fills the viewport ahead.
She looks at it for a moment. Just the planet. Just the light of it.
Then she looks at herself in the small mirror to her left. The scarf arranged properly for the first time since she was a child. She looks at it for a moment.
Then she looks away.
Flies.
**Sihnon — House Madrassa — Approach**
She wasn’t prepared for how beautiful it would be.
That’s the first thing. The only thing for a moment. House Madrassa sits on a rise above the city, which is already beautiful, which makes the house extraordinary. Pale stone the colour of early morning. Gardens terraced down the hillside in concentric rings, each planted with something different — flowers she has no names for, trees shaped over decades into forms that look natural and aren’t, water running in channels cut precisely into the stone.
The shuttle’s transponder pings.
A voice comes back almost before the ping has finished.
LANDING CONTROL
Serenity shuttle, you are cleared for landing. Berth four. Welcome to House Madrassa.
Saff doesn’t respond.
Just looks at the house through the viewport.
Sets her jaw.
Brings the shuttle down.

*Three — The Garden*

**House Madrassa — The Gardens — Continuous**
The garden smells of incense and something green and living underneath it. The kind of smell that gets into clothing and stays there.
Saff stands at the bottom of the shuttle ramp and takes it in.
The grounds stretch away from the landing berth in tiered levels — stone paths worn smooth by years of feet, gardens planted with the kind of careful intention that has been tended and adjusted across generations until it has started to look effortless. Companions move through the paths in ones and twos, unhurried, their robes catching the light. Nobody runs here. Nobody raises their voice. The house manages its own atmosphere the way it manages everything — without appearing to.
A young Companion is already coming toward her across the lower terrace. Seventeen, maybe eighteen. The kind of welcoming expression that has been trained into her so thoroughly it looks entirely natural.
Saff watches her come.
She clocks the moment the girl’s eyes find the scarf. The fractional pause. The recovery. Imperceptible to anyone who hasn’t been taught what to look for.
The girl reaches her and inclines her head with the specific grace of someone performing a welcome she has been asked to perform.
LIAN
Welcome to House Madrassa. My name is Lian. May I ask your name?
Saff looks at her for a moment. At the warmth in her face. At the standing orders behind the warmth.
SAFF
My name is Saff Reynolds. Inara Serra is my mother. I imagine someone here has been expecting me.
Lian’s expression doesn’t change. But something behind it does — the micro-adjustment of someone receiving information they were told to expect and activating instructions they were told to follow.
She inclines her head.
LIAN
Please. Come with me.
She turns and walks back toward the house.
Saff follows.
As they pass through the lower garden an older Companion glances up from a bench. A teacher, by the look of her. Her eyes go to the scarf.
Stay there a beat too long.
Then she looks away.
Saff keeps walking.
The house opens around her as they climb — archways, courtyards, the sound of water somewhere. Beautiful in a way that is entirely deliberate. She has been taught about that too.
Lian leads her through a side corridor and through a door into a smaller room. Not a grand reception. Something more intimate. A low table. Tea already laid. A single chair on the far side already occupied.
The woman in the chair is perhaps sixty. She has the bearing of someone who has been comfortable in rooms of power for a very long time. She is looking at Saff with the specific expression of a person who has been waiting for something and has now decided how they feel about it arriving.
Her name is YUSARA.
She gestures to the chair opposite.
YUSARA
Please sit. You must be tired from your journey.
Saff sits.
She picks up the tea.
Drinks.
Watches Yusara over the rim of the cup.

*Four — The Tea*

**House Madrassa — The Private Room — Continuous**
Yusara pours for herself. Doesn’t offer more to Saff. A small thing. Deliberate.
YUSARA
We don’t receive many unannounced visitors. Especially not arriving on shuttles that haven’t been in service for twenty years.
SAFF
The shuttle works fine.
Yusara smiles. It reaches her eyes just far enough to be convincing.
YUSARA
I’m sure it does. Your mother always maintained her things well.
Saff drinks her tea. Says nothing.
YUSARA
You’ll want to see her.
SAFF
That’s why I’m here.
YUSARA
Of course. These things take a little time to arrange. High Priestess Serra has a full schedule. I’m sure you understand.
Saff sets her cup down.
SAFF
Of course.
They look at each other across the low table. Two people having a conversation that is entirely about something neither of them is saying. The tea gets cold. Neither of them notices or mentions it.
YUSARA
How long have you been out in the verse? On your own.
SAFF
A while.
YUSARA
Your mother’s people are rim world people, I understand. Running jobs. That kind of life.
SAFF
That kind of life.
Yusara tilts her head very slightly. Reassessing something.
YUSARA
You have her training.
Not a question. Saff doesn’t answer it.
A beat. Then the door opens.
Three guards. Alliance issue body armour. They don’t draw weapons. They don’t need to. They just stand in the doorway in the specific way that makes the doorway unavailable.
Saff looks at them. Looks at Yusara. Yusara has the grace to look almost regretful.
YUSARA
You have your mother’s composure. I always admired that about her.
Saff stands up.
Straightens her jacket.
Walks to the door.
The guards fall in around her without touching her. The corridor outside is longer than the one she came in on. She counts the doors. Notes the turns. Reads the light coming through the windows to establish direction.
She has time. She uses it.
**House Madrassa — A Cell — Minutes Later**
The room is small. Clean. A bench along one wall. A window too high and too narrow to be useful. The door closes behind her with the specific sound of a lock that means business.
Saff sits on the bench.
She looks at the door.
Then at the window.
Then at her hands.
She starts going through everything she saw on the way in. The garden layout. The number of Companions she passed and where. The corridor that turned left toward what looked like a residential wing. The guard post she clocked through a doorway. The window in the private room that faced east.
She does this methodically, without hurrying, the way her father’s daughter would and the way her mother’s training taught her.
Both of them useful. Neither of them enough on their own.
She finishes going through what she knows.
Sits back.
Waits.

*Five — The Message*

**House Madrassa — The Rival’s Chambers — Same Time**
Yusara is back at her tea.
A different room. Larger. The kind of room that says something about the person who occupies it without them having to say anything themselves. She sits at the window looking out over the gardens below with the unhurried quality of someone who has already moved on to the next thing in their mind.
A knock. A subordinate enters and inclines her head.
SUBORDINATE
The message has been delivered.
Yusara nods. Doesn’t look up from the garden.
YUSARA
Thank you.
The subordinate withdraws. The door closes.
Yusara takes a sip of her tea.
Looks out over the garden.
Waits.
**House Madrassa — Inara’s Chambers — Same Time**
The chambers are quiet. The light through the silk hangings the same quality of light it has always been in rooms Inara inhabits — warm, deliberate, the product of twenty years of knowing exactly how a space should feel.
A knock at the inner door. One of her advisers enters — a woman who has been in this house longer than Yusara downstairs and knows every corridor of its politics. She crosses to Inara and says two sentences.
We don’t hear them.
We see Inara’s face.
She is still for a moment. Just a moment. Then something settles in her expression — not anger, not fear, something older and quieter than either of those things. The specific stillness of a person who has been waiting for an enemy to make their move and is now entirely clear on what happens next.
She says something to the adviser. The adviser nods and leaves.
Inara crosses to the far wall.
She knows where everything is without looking.
The bow comes off the wall first. Not ceremonially. Not carefully. The way something comes off a wall that has been taken down before. She checks the string with her thumb. Satisfied.
The swords next. Two of them, stacked on a low bracket beside the bow. Short enough for corridors. She takes them down one at a time and checks each edge.
She sets everything on the low table and crosses to the wardrobe.
The robe comes out first. Dark. Simple. Not her robes of office — something older, something that predates the title. She dresses in it without hurrying. Each fastening done correctly the first time.
Then the armour. Fitted leather, shaped to her body over years of adjustment, worn over the robe. It goes on in a specific order that her hands remember without being asked. Each piece settled before the next one begins.
The sword harness last. The two scabbards stack on her left hip. She sheathes both blades.
The quiver goes on last. Right side. Tall. The arrows separated with purpose, each one placed deliberately. Nothing casual. Nothing wasted.
She picks up the bow.
Looks at herself once in the mirror.
Not the High Priestess.
She doesn’t look back when she leaves.
The door closes.
The room is exactly as it was.

*Six — There’s No Time*

**House Madrassa — The Cell — Later**
The cell hasn’t changed. The bench. The high narrow window. The door with its serious lock.
SAFF is exactly where she was. Sitting. Waiting. She has been through everything she knows three times now and the fourth pass isn’t going to tell her anything new.
The lock turns.
The door opens.
Not guards.
INARA.
She is standing in the doorway in a dark robe with armour over it and a bow in her left hand and a quiver on her right side and twenty years of distance between her and the daughter she is looking at for the first time.
One second. Maybe two.
Then:
INARA
There’s no time.
She steps back from the doorway.
Saff stands up.
She is halfway to the door when the guards come from the left corridor — three of them, fast, already reaching for weapons.
Inara turns.
Her right hand goes to the quiver. Three arrows. Three draws. Her left hand barely moves.
Three guards. Three seconds. None of them reach their weapons.
The corridor is quiet.
Inara lowers the bow.
Looks at Saff.
INARA  (moving)
Now there’s no time.
Saff follows.

*Seven — The Corridor*

**House Madrassa — The Corridors — Continuous**
The next corridor is narrower.
Inara clocks it in the same instant she clocks the two guards coming from the far end. She drops the bow without breaking stride.
Both hands find the hilts. The blades come free in a single sweeping arc — fluid, unhurried, like the beginning of something rather than the end.
The first guard doesn’t understand what has happened until it is already over.
The second understands. Steps back. Raises his weapon.
Inara reverses the upper blade and holds it out to her left without looking.
INARA
Take it.
Saff takes it.
The second guard looks at two women with blades in a corridor that has become very small and makes a decision.
He steps aside.
They move.
**House Madrassa — The Central Hall — Continuous**
The corridor opens into something larger.
The Hall is high-ceilinged, stone-floored, the kind of space that was built to impress and currently contains approximately a dozen of Yusara’s supporters in various states of readiness. YUSARA stands at the far end. She has had time to assemble her people. She was expecting this.
Inara and Saff come through the archway and stop.
The arithmetic is obvious. Two against twelve. Yusara almost looks apologetic.
YUSARA
You’ve always been remarkable, Inara. But this is —
The door on the left opens.
And the door on the right.
Inara’s people come through both simultaneously. Not students. Not junior Companions. People who have been in this house longer than Yusara’s faction and have been waiting for exactly this instruction. They fill the room in the specific way that changes the arithmetic entirely.
Yusara looks at the room.
Looks at Inara.
Looks at the room again.
She is too intelligent to die today over odds that have just reversed themselves. She lowers her hand. Her people follow.
The Hall is very quiet.
INARA  (to Yusara, passing her without stopping)
This conversation isn’t finished.
Yusara says nothing.
Inara and Saff walk out.

*Eight — Mal*

**House Madrassa — The Outer Garden — Moments Later**
They come out through a side gate into the lower garden. Away from the hall. Away from Yusara’s people. The incense smell is fainter here. The air cooler. The sound of water from somewhere below.
They stop.
For a moment neither of them says anything. The fight is behind them and ahead of them is everything else and neither of them knows how to start.
Inara looks at her daughter. At the silk scarf. At the tactical vest underneath the armour she is holding. At the specific way she stands — weight forward, ready, the way Mal stands.
She asks about the training. Not because she doesn’t know — she has known, from a careful distance, for years. But it was the last moment she had a fixed point. The last time she knew exactly where Saff was in the verse.
INARA
The training. How far did you get.
SAFF
Far enough to walk out.
Inara nods. Something in her face settles.
A beat.
INARA
Mal.
Just his name. Everything it carries.
SAFF
He’s fine.
Inara nods again. Looks at her hands for a moment. Then back up.
They talked for a while after that, in the way that people talk when twenty years is too much to cover and they’ve both decided to start from where they are instead. Practical things mostly. The verse. The ship. What Saff has been doing. Inara asking questions and Saff answering them in minimum words and both of them understanding that the minimum words are carrying a great deal more than they appear to.
At some point Inara almost smiles.
At some point Saff almost lets her.
Neither of them names what this is. They don’t need to.
Then Inara looks up at the sky above the garden. At the quality of the light. At something she is calculating.
INARA
You need to go.
SAFF
I know.
INARA
That ship cannot be in Core space. Not that ship. If the wrong person sees her —
SAFF
I know.
Inara looks at her for a moment. Then she reaches into her robe and produces a small card. Sets it in Saff’s hand.
INARA
New Melbourne. There’s a public wave board. When you see a listing for —
She says the name of the listing. We don’t hear it. Just Saff’s face as she commits it.
INARA
PO Box 1167. That’s me.
Saff closes her hand around the card.
They look at each other.
There is no embrace. There is the specific charged stillness of two people who are standing close enough and neither of them moves and both of them know why and neither of them says so.
Then Saff turns and walks back toward the landing berth.
Inara watches her go.
At the gate Saff stops. Doesn’t turn around.
SAFF  (quietly, to the gate)
He misses you.
She walks through it.
Inara stands in the garden for a moment after she’s gone.
Then she turns and walks back into the house. She has a great deal to manage.

*Nine — She Just Had Something To Do*

**The Ship — Cargo Bay — Later**
The shuttle redocks with a sound that makes everyone in the cargo bay exhale without meaning to.
CAS is already there. PIP is already there. JAYNE is already there, arms crossed, hat on, the specific expression of a man who has been furious for several hours and has decided what he thinks about how this ended.
The hatch opens.
SAFF comes down the ramp.
She is still wearing the silk scarf. Still arranged the way it was meant to be worn. She is also carrying a sword that isn’t hers and has a cut on her left forearm that she hasn’t mentioned to anyone and isn’t going to.
She reaches the bottom of the ramp. Looks at the three of them.
SAFF
Thank you for waiting.
A beat.
SAFF
I just had something to do.
Jayne looks at her for a long moment. At the sword. At the cut on her arm. At the scarf.
He doesn’t say anything.
He turns and walks back inside.
Pip looks at the sword. Looks at her arm. Opens his mouth.
SAFF
I’m fine.
PIP
I have a med kit.
SAFF
I’m fine, Pip.
Pip closes his mouth. Then opens it again.
PIP
The med kit is very good.
Cas is already moving toward the cockpit.
CAS  (over her shoulder)
We need to go.
Saff follows.
Pip follows Saff, med kit already in hand.

*Ten — Full Burn*

**The Ship — The Cockpit — Continuous**
CAS drops into the pilot’s chair and her hands are already moving across the board.
On the long range sensors, large and slow and entirely confident in its authority, an Alliance cruiser sits between them and open space. It has been sitting there for a while. It has been watching the Serenity shuttle come and go. It has been patient.
Its patience has run out.
The comms crackle.
ALLIANCE COMMS
Unregistered Firefly-class vessel. You are in restricted Core space. Cut your engines and prepare to be boarded.
Cas looks at the cruiser on the sensor display. At its size. At its heading. At the specific geometry of a very large ship that has been sitting still for a long time and cannot change that quickly.
She sets their heading.
Straight at the cruiser.
SAFF  (from the doorway)
Cas.
CAS
I see it.
SAFF
You’re pointed at an Alliance cruiser.
CAS
I know.
The cruiser fills the cockpit glass as Serenity accelerates toward it. On the Alliance bridge someone is running the arithmetic. A Firefly isn’t a threat. Except this one is coming straight at them and isn’t slowing down and the officer has approximately eight seconds.
Seven.
Six.
The cruiser begins to turn.
There it is.
The moment it commits to the turn Cas cuts the pulse drive. The ship goes quiet for two seconds — just thrusters, just reaction drive, Serenity pivoting on her own axis with the specific ease of a ship that has been doing this for thirty years and knows exactly how.
Ten seconds. Maybe less.
New heading. Away from the cruiser. Into open space. Cas hits full burn.
The tail section blooms gold.
The gap between them opens.
The cruiser is still turning when Serenity clears Core space and the black opens up ahead of them and the stars are the same stars they have always been and the ship hums and the gold fades and she moves.
Cas sits back.
Lets out a long slow breath.
Looks at the stegosaurus on the console.
Says nothing.
**Deep Space — Exterior**
The ship small against the stars. The gold already fading from her tail. The black taking her back.
She moves.
She always moves.
— END OF EPISODE FIVE —
“Same sky. Different dust. Still flying.”
