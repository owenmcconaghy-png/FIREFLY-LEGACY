<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">Deep Space — The Ship — Before Dawn</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She moves through the black the way she always has.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Unhurried. Sure of herself. Older than anything else currently flying in this quadrant and quietly aware of it. Her engines turn at a low, settled frequency — not the ragged catch-and-steady of last night but something that has found its rhythm now and is content to keep it. The sound moves through her hull the way a heartbeat moves through a body. Present. Constant. Not something you notice until you start listening.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The stars outside her ports are the same stars they have always been.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Inside her it is almost dark. The running lights throw long amber shapes across the corridor walls. The recycled air smells like metal and old wood and something underneath both of those things — something that has no name but that anyone who has ever lived aboard a ship for long enough would recognise. It is the smell of a place that has been lived in. That has absorbed the particular accumulation of people and time and small ordinary moments until it carries them the way old furniture carries them. Quietly. Permanently.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Four people are asleep somewhere inside her.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Or almost asleep.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Or trying.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">The Ship — Cas’s Bunk — Same Time</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">CAS lies on her back in a narrow bunk with his eyes open.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The ceiling above her is close. There are scratches in the metal that someone made a long time ago — not damage, not wear, just the absent-minded marks of someone who spent a lot of time lying in this exact spot looking at this exact ceiling and thinking. She traces one of them with her eye without moving his head.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She has been lying here for an hour.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She is thinking about Decker.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Not dramatically. Not the way grief announces itself. Just the quiet persistent presence of a name in a room where it doesn’t belong yet. She moves it around. Examines it from different angles. Sets it down and it comes back. She does not fight this. She has learned that fighting it makes it louder.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She thinks about Rex. The four steps and then the nothing.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She thinks about Lena. The wrong turn. The specific way she didn’t make a sound.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She puts these three things in a row and looks at them. The plan was good. She knows the plan was good. He has gone over it enough times now to be certain of that much at least. The plan was good and these things happened anyway and somewhere in the gap between those two facts is something he is going to have to live with.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She exhales.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Long. Slow. The way his mother taught him to exhale when a situation requires the next thing from you before you are ready to give it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She gets up.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">The Ship — The Cockpit — Minutes Later</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She comes here because he doesn’t know where else to go and the cockpit is the part of any ship that makes most sense to him.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She sits in the pilot’s chair.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">It fits differently than the Eleanor’s chair. More worn. The cushion has taken the shape of someone who sat here for years and that shape is not her shape. She settles into it anyway. Finds the edges of it. She has always been able to find the edges of a chair.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The controls are dark. The ship is flying herself on the course she set before she slept — or tried to sleep — and she seems comfortable with this. Competent, even. Like she has done it before.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She looks at the dashboard.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She notices it in the way you notice something that has been there the whole time and suddenly becomes visible.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Taped to the lower left corner of the console, slightly above the proximity alert, almost hidden under the edge of the display housing.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">A small plastic dinosaur.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Green. Worn smooth at the edges. A stegosaurus.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Someone taped it there a very long time ago.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Cas looks at it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She reaches out.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Doesn’t touch it. Just reaches. Hand stopping an inch short. Hovering there for a moment in the amber light.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Then he sees the initials.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Scratched into the console housing just beside it. Small. Careful. Not carved in anger or boredom but placed there deliberately, with intention, the way you mark something you want to stay.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">H.W.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She sits very still.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Outside, the stars move in their slow indifferent way across the cockpit glass. The ship hums. The proximity alert light blinks its steady green.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Cas sits in the chair that doesn’t fit her shape and looks at two small letters scratched into a console and does not move for a very long time.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">The Ship — The Engine Room — Same Time</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">PIP found the engine room forty minutes ago.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He has not left.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He told himself he was checking the compression coils — a reasonable thing to do on a new ship, an entirely professional thing to do, nothing strange about it — and he is, technically, still checking the compression coils. But he has been checking the same compression coil for approximately thirty-five minutes and it became clear some time ago that the compression coil is not really what he’s doing.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">What he’s doing is listening.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The engine room of this ship has a particular quality of sound that he has never encountered before. Not louder or quieter or more or less mechanical than other engine rooms he has worked in. Just different in a way he cannot yet name. The frequencies sit differently. The harmonics resolve in a way that makes the back of his neck feel like something has just said his name in a room full of people.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He moves through the space slowly, trailing his fingers along the surfaces. The pipes. The housings. The curves of old metal worn smooth by years of hands that knew what they were touching.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He stops at the main compression housing.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">There is something on it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He leans closer. The running light from above throws an amber shadow across the surface and he has to angle his head to see it properly.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">A small brass plate. Bolted flush to the housing. The kind of thing you have made when you want something to last.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He wipes the dust from it with his thumb.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The letters come clear.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">PROPERTY OF K. FRYE-TAM.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">IF YOU TOUCH THIS WITHOUT A WRENCH I’LL BITE YOU.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Pip reads it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Reads it again.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">His hand is still resting on the housing. He becomes aware of this slowly, the way you become aware of something that was already true before you noticed it. His hand on the metal. The metal warm in a way that metal left alone should not be warm.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He does not move his hand.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The engine hums around him. Under him. Through him. The same sound it has always made in this room, the sound that has nothing to do with fuel or combustion or mechanical process, the sound that is just her.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">His expression doesn’t change.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">That is the tell. When Pip’s expression doesn’t change it means something very large is happening somewhere he can’t quite reach yet. His face goes very still and his eyes go very somewhere-else and the engine hums and the amber light falls across the brass plate and his mother’s name.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He stays like that for a long time.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">The Ship — Inara’s Shuttle — Same Time</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">SAFF almost walked past the hatch.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She is not a person who wanders. She moves through spaces with purpose — from here to there, for this reason, on this schedule. Wandering is what happens when you don’t have a plan and Saff Reynolds has always had a plan.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">But the ship is strange to her in the way that only very old things can be strange. Not threatening. Not unwelcoming. Just layered in a way that makes her slow down without meaning to. Like walking through a place where a lot of living has happened and the air still carries traces of it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She almost walked past the hatch.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Then she stopped.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">It is a shuttle hatch — one of two set into the port side of the ship, the kind that opens onto a small self-contained vessel that can separate from the main body and travel independently. The hatch is closed but not sealed. The light around the frame has a different quality to the amber running lights of the corridor — warmer, coming from inside. She stands in the corridor for a moment. She is aware that she is standing in the corridor. She is aware that she has no particular reason to open it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She opens it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The shuttle is small. A bunk built into one wall. A low table. A mirror on the opposite side with the silver worn at the edges. And silk.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Hangings on the curved walls in deep red and gold, pinned carefully to the metal, fine enough that the recycled air from the vent above makes them move. Barely. Just enough. They have been here long enough that the colours have faded at the top where the light hits them and deepened at the bottom where it doesn’t.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Saff stands in the hatchway.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She grew up in rooms like this. Not identical to this — the rooms of her childhood were larger, more deliberate, arranged with the specific intentionality of a Companion’s training house — but the same register. The same quality of care applied to the space. The same understanding that a room can be an argument for a particular kind of life.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She crosses to the low table.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">There is a small wooden box on it. Square. Dark with age. A simple brass clasp that has been opened and closed enough times that it moves without resistance.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She doesn’t open it immediately.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She looks at it. She picks up the dust on the lid with one finger and examines it. She sets the finger down. She looks at the silk hangings. She looks at her own reflection in the spotted mirror — tactical vest, the silk scarf around her neck that she has worn every day since she was sixteen and has never once explained to anyone — and she looks at the box again.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She opens it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Inside: a dried flower.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Saffron. The particular faded orange of something that was once bright and has kept its shape through the drying.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Under it: a note. Folded once. Small, neat handwriting that she has seen before on letters she was given as a child and told to keep safe and did.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She does not unfold it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Not yet.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She just holds it in both hands and stands in the middle of the shuttle and the silk moves in the recycled air and somewhere deep in the ship the engine hums and the saffron flower sits in its box and Saff Reynolds stands very still with her mother’s handwriting in her hands and does not make a sound.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">The Ship — Various — Continuous</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">CAS leaves the cockpit the way he came in — quietly, with his hands in his pockets and his eyes doing something complicated that his face has decided not to show.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He moves through the corridor without particular direction. Left at the junction because left felt right. Down the short passage toward the mid-section. The ship makes sounds around him as he walks — small adjustments, the tick of metal expanding and contracting, the particular sigh of the heating system cycling — and he listens to them the way he was listening to the controls earlier. Not analysing. Just present.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He turns a corner.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">SAFF is standing in the corridor.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She is facing away from him, looking at something on the wall — a bracket, or a seam, or nothing at all, it’s impossible to tell. She hears him and turns.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">They look at each other.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Neither of them says anything.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">There is a moment — three seconds, maybe four — in which two people who have been through something together and have not yet processed it stand in a corridor and acknowledge each other’s existence without knowing what words belong to the situation.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Saff gives a very small nod.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Cas gives one back.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She turns and walks.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">After a beat he follows. Not because she invited him. Because left still feels right and she went left.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">The Ship — Lower Passage — Same Time</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">PIP is not lost.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He would like to be clear about that. He is not lost. He simply took a corridor that went somewhere he didn’t expect and is now in a part of the ship he hasn’t been in yet which is completely different from being lost because he could find his way back to the engine room any time he wanted.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He is just not going back to the engine room yet.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The lower passage is narrow and low-ceilinged and has the specific quality of a space that was designed for function rather than habitation — pipes running along the upper wall, junction boxes at intervals, the kind of utilitarian honesty that Pip finds more comfortable than rooms that try to be something. He trails his fingers along the pipes as he walks. Reads the temperature of them. The pressure. The particular language of a ship talking to herself.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He rounds a corner.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">JAYNE is standing in the middle of the passage.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He is not doing anything. He is simply standing there in the way that large men sometimes stand in narrow spaces — taking up most of it, apparently unbothered by this fact, looking at a junction box on the wall with an expression that suggests either deep thought or complete absence of thought and from the outside these are indistinguishable.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Pip stops.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Jayne doesn’t move.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">They look at each other.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Sorry. I wasn’t — I was just —</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He gestures vaguely in the direction he was going, which is the direction Jayne is blocking.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Jayne looks at the gesture. Looks at Pip. Looks back at the junction box.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">You know where you’re going?</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP  (pause)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Yes.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP  (shorter pause)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">No.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Not really.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Jayne considers this for a moment with the gravity of a man who has been asked a significant question.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Then he turns and walks back the way he came, which is the direction Pip was trying to go, apparently entirely comfortable with this reversal, offering no explanation.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Pip watches him go.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Then follows.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Because he is also going that way. Probably.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">The Ship — Outside The Mess Room — Moments Later</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The four of them arrive at approximately the same time from three different directions.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">This is not planned. Ships just do this sometimes — arrange their geometry so that certain rooms collect people whether the people intended to go there or not. The mess room of this ship has always had that quality. It has collected people for thirty years and it has not lost the habit.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">CAS and SAFF come from the starboard corridor.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">JAYNE and PIP come from the lower passage, Jayne first, Pip two steps behind him with the expression of someone who has been following a large man through a ship for five minutes and has still not entirely worked out how this happened.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">They all stop outside the mess room door at the same moment.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Four people. Three doorway-widths of space. The specific social geometry of strangers who have simultaneously arrived at the same destination.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Jayne looks at all three of them.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE  (walking in)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Well.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The others follow.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Because what else do you do.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">The Ship — The Mess Room — Continuous</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The mess room is the warmest room on the ship.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Not because of the heating — the heating on this ship is eccentric, warmest in the corridors and coolest in the sleeping quarters, which is the wrong way around and suggests someone at some point made a modification they never got around to reversing. The warmth of the mess room is something else. The warmth of a room that has had a great many people in it over a long time doing ordinary things. Eating. Arguing. Sitting in silence. The warmth that rooms absorb from living and give back slowly long after the living has moved on.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">There is a table. Real wood, worn smooth, long enough to seat three a side. The ceiling is higher than you expect — unexpectedly generous for a ship this size, with observation windows set into the upper walls that let in the ambient glow of running lights from the deck above. A galley alcove at the far end smelling faintly of old coffee and reconstituted protein and something underneath both of those that is just the smell of the room itself.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Jayne sits in the chair in the corner. The one slightly back from the table, slightly in shadow. He sits in it the way the chair was designed to be sat in — as though the chair expected him specifically.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Cas sits at the end of the table closest to the far wall. She puts both hands flat on the table and looks at them.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Saff sits opposite. She has the wooden box from the shuttle in one hand. She sets it on the table. Looks at it. Doesn’t open it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Pip sits last. He pulls his chair in. Looks at the table surface. Starts to look at the room. Stops looking at the room. Looks back at the table.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Nobody speaks.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The ship hums.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The amber light from above moves slowly across the table the way light moves when the ship adjusts her heading. It is the only thing in the room that moves.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Jayne has a mug of something in front of him that was already there when they arrived. Nobody asks when he made it or how he knew to be here first. Some things about Jayne Cobb are simply true in the way that weather is true — present, not requiring explanation.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The silence goes on.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">It is not an uncomfortable silence. That is the strange thing about it. Four people who do not know each other sitting in a room they have no claim to, in a ship they cannot explain, after a night that should have broken them, and the silence is not uncomfortable. It is the silence of people who are all thinking about the same thing and know it and are waiting for someone to begin.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">PIP is looking at the table surface.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">At the marks in it. The rings. The burn at the far corner. The grain of the wood coming through where the finish has worn.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">And the letters.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He sees them the way he saw the nameplate — not looking for them, just suddenly aware of them, the way you become aware of something that was always there.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">J. COBB.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Carved into the wood near his side of the table. Not deep. Careful. The kind of thing bored hands make when the ship is quiet and the black outside is the same black it always is and you have a knife and nowhere particular to be.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She reaches out.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Runs his finger along the letters.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Once.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The sound of a fingertip on old carved wood. Barely a sound at all. In the quiet of the mess room it is very loud.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Jayne sets his mug down.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The knife comes out smooth and unhurried. Laid flat on the table. He taps the blade once on the letters.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The sound of a knife on old carved wood.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Then he picks up his mug again.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The silence continues.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Cas is very still.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Saff is looking at the box.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The ship hums.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Low and steady and patient in the way that things are patient when they have been waiting a very long time and have learned that waiting is just another kind of being.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">It is PIP who speaks first.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Not because he has decided to. Just because he is constitutionally unable to sit in the presence of something true and leave it unnamed. It is the same instinct that spun him around on a barstool to correct a man he’d never met about compression coils. The same instinct that put his ear against a vault wall and listened until the wall told him what it wanted.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He is not looking at anyone when he says it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Just at the table. At the marks in it. At the carved letters under his finger.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP  (quietly)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Someone lived here.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The words sit in the room.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Nobody responds immediately. But the quality of the silence changes — the way the quality of a room changes when a window is opened, nothing dramatic, just a shift in the air that means the inside and the outside have become connected.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">On this ship. For a long time. Someone really lived here.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He looks up.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">At Jayne.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Jayne is looking at his mug.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE  (after a long moment)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Yeah.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Lot of people did.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The ship hums.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The amber light moves slowly across the table.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Inside the mess room something has shifted. Something that was held separately by four people has become, quietly and without announcement, a thing held together. Not trust exactly. Not yet. Just the first acknowledgement that they are all in the same room with the same questions and the same ghosts and nobody is leaving.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Pip looks back at the letters.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Then at Jayne.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Is there coffee on this boat.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Jayne almost smiles.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Almost.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">There’s always coffee on this boat.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Pip gets up.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Goes to the galley.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The sound of cupboards being opened and investigated with the specific exploratory energy of someone getting to know a kitchen for the first time. A clatter. A pause.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Then:</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP  (from inside the cupboard, with quiet reverence)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">There’s a mug in here with a dinosaur on it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">CAS goes very still.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">JAYNE looks at the ceiling.</p>

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">The Ship — The Mess Room — Continuous</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Pip is looking at the carved letters.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Jayne is looking at all three of them with the expression of a man who has been dreading this moment for twenty years and is surprised to find it feels less like drowning and more like putting something very heavy down.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The others find him under the main housing with his legs sticking out and his diagnostic tool making small precise sounds against the engine’s underside.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">From under the housing, the sound of Pip working. Not frantic. Methodical. The occasional soft spoken observation to himself that is less communication and more thinking out loud.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP  (murmuring)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">There you are. Hello. Yeah that’s — that’s not great. How long have you been doing that. Long time probably. Nobody to notice. That’s not your fault.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Then at the engine.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Of course you do.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">You want the part or not.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">We want the part.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Broker I’ve used before. Operates out of Rattle Rock Station — third moon of the sixth planet out from Huang Xing. Rim side. Doesn’t ask questions.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">CAS</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Will they have the part.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE  (a beat)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">They’ll know where to get it. That’s different from having it but it’s close enough.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">It’ll cost. Parts like that don’t come cheap. We’ll need coin.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The four of them consider this.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The ship hums. Slightly irregular now that they’re all listening for it. The rattle underneath.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">The broker. Do they deal in jobs as well as parts.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Jayne looks at her.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">What kind of question is that.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">The kind where I already know the answer and want to hear you say it.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Jayne picks up his mug. Finds it empty. Sets it down.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">JAYNE</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Yeah. They deal in jobs.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Saff looks at Cas. Cas looks at Pip. Pip looks at the engine.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">I can get us there. How long do we have before the mount gives completely —</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He looks at the engine. Runs a hand along the housing. Listens.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Four days. Maybe five if she’s careful with herself.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He says this directly to the engine. Not to the room.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Saff makes a decision. The room feels it before she says it — the specific shift in her posture that Pip has started to recognise as the moment Saff Reynolds stops considering and starts doing.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">SAFF</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Set the course.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Nobody asks who she’s talking to.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">CAS is already moving toward the cockpit.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">The Ship — Cockpit — Later</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Cas drops into the pilot’s chair and pulls up the nav system. The interface is old but responsive — the ship knows what she’s doing even when her pilot doesn’t yet.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She finds Huang Xing on the chart. Traces out to the sixth planet. Three moons marked. The third one small and unremarkable and exactly the kind of place that exists because someone needed it to and not for any other reason.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">RATTLE ROCK STATION. Population: unregistered. Alliance jurisdiction: nominal.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She sets the course.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The ship adjusts. Smoothly. Willingly. Like she already knew where they were going.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Cas watches the stars shift through the cockpit glass as she comes around to her new heading. Her hands are loose on the controls. Not gripping. Just present. The way her father’s hands must have looked in this same chair.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She doesn’t think about that.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She looks at the stegosaurus on the console.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">CAS  (quietly, to the ship, to the stegosaurus, to nobody)</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Alright.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The same word he said last night on the Eleanor. Different ship. Different chair. Different everything.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Same word.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">Deep Space — Exterior — Same Time</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Outside, in the black, the ship moves.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She is small against the stars. Grey and scarred and improbable, the kind of vessel that looks like it has no business still flying and flies anyway. As she comes around to her new heading her tail section begins to glow.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Slowly. Warm amber deepening to gold, blooming from the rear of the ship like something waking up, spreading steady and certain into the dark around her.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">A firefly in the black.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She leaves a brief trail of light behind her as she goes. It fades. The black takes it back.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She doesn’t need it to stay.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She already knows where she’s going.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">The Ship — Various — The Next Four Days</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Four days is a long time to be on a ship with people you don’t know.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">It is also, it turns out, exactly enough time to start knowing them.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Not fully. Not deeply. But enough. The specific knowledge that comes from sharing a kitchen and a corridor and a bathroom with imperfect scheduling and the particular intimacies of a ship where there is nowhere to be alone that isn’t also somewhere else. You learn things about people on ships that you would never learn anywhere else. How they take their coffee. How loud they are in the morning. Whether they put things back where they found them.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Pip does not put things back where he found them because he cannot see the logic of the original system and has quietly reorganised the galley according to a different logic that makes more sense to him personally. This causes a specific expression on Saff’s face that he has started to catalogue.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Cas sleeps badly and is quiet about it. She is in the cockpit most nights in the small hours, not flying, just sitting. Sometimes with the stegosaurus in her hand. Sometimes not.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Saff runs drills at six in the morning in the cargo bay that nobody asked her to run and nobody is invited to join. Pip joins anyway on the second day and she doesn’t tell him to leave.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Jayne fixes things. Quietly, without being asked, he works through the ship’s systems one by one — a sticky door mechanism here, a faulty light fitting there, a water recycler that has been running at sixty percent efficiency for what looks like several years. He doesn’t announce these repairs. He just does them. The ship responds the way old things respond to being cared for — gradually, almost reluctantly, like she’d forgotten what it felt like.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">On the third day Pip finds a set of hand-written maintenance notes tucked behind the primary coupling housing. Small pages covered in neat looping handwriting with little diagrams in the margins. He reads three pages before he has to stop.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">He doesn’t tell anyone about them.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Not yet.</p>


<hr style="border:none; border-top:1px solid #8B2500; margin:2em 0;">

<p style="color:#555555; font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; text-align:left; margin-top:0.83em; margin-bottom:0.25em; ">The Ship — Cockpit — Day Four</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">CAS sees it first.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">On the edge of the long range sensors. A presence. Massive and slow and ringed in the particular way of a gas giant that has been there since before anyone had names for things like this.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">She watches it grow on the screen for a long time before he says anything.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Then he opens the ship-wide.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">CAS</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">Coming up on Huang Xing Six. Twenty minutes to approach vector.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The sound of movement through the ship. Feet on metal. The particular quality of four people who have been in transit for four days and are ready to be somewhere.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">They gather in the cockpit. Not enough room for all of them properly — Jayne takes up most of the available space just by existing — but nobody goes back to their bunk. They press in.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">And there it is.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The planet fills the cockpit window slowly, the way very large things reveal themselves slowly — the rings first, catching the distant sun, then the bands of colour in the atmosphere below, deep ochre and rust and something almost purple at the poles. Three moons in various points of their orbits.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The third one small and dark and unremarkable.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">A cluster of lights on its surface that might be a settlement or might be something else.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Rattle Rock Station.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Pip leans forward between the seats, hands on the headrests, and looks at the planet with the expression of someone who has just been reminded that the verse is enormous and they are very small in it and this is somehow not a frightening thought but an exciting one.</p>

<p style="margin-top:0.5em; margin-bottom:0; text-align:left;"><span style="color:#8B2500; font-weight:bold;">PIP</span></p>
<p style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:0.5em; text-align:left;">That’s something.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Nobody disagrees.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The ship drops toward her approach vector. The rings catch the light differently as the angle changes — gold to silver to something that has no name, the way colours sometimes don’t in space where there’s nothing to compare them to.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">The rattle is still there underneath the hum.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Patient. Waiting.</p>

<p style="font-size:15px; font-weight:normal; text-align:left; margin-top:0.33em; margin-bottom:0.33em; ">Four days was enough.</p>

<p style="color:#8B2500; font-size:19px; font-weight:bold; text-align:center; margin-top:2.0em; margin-bottom:2.0em; ">— END OF EPISODE TWO —</p>
