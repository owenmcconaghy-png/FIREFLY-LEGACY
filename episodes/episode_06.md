*One — The Wave*

**The Ship — The Mess Room — Morning**
PIP is at the stove. Whatever he’s making smells considerably better than it has any right to.
JAYNE is already at the table with a plate and the hat and the particular quality of stillness that means he got there first and considers this a personal achievement. CAS has her mug. SAFF has her coffee. Nobody is talking because nobody needs to.
This is what four weeks on a ship looks like. The comfortable kind of quiet.
The Cortex chime breaks it.
Ship-wide. Not the helm, not a personal wave — the kind of call that goes to whoever answers. Cas reaches across and opens the channel.
CAS
Serenity.

CONTACT  (over comms)

CAS

CONTACT  (over comms)
Got something that needs doing. Border world. Dead moon off Jiangyin. Cache of Browncoat munitions been sitting in the ground for twenty-four years. Rifles. Charges. Ordnance. Alliance is sweeping the sector next month. Someone needs it moved before they find it and use it to roll up everyone still connected to the Independent cause.
A beat.
CONTACT  (over comms)
People who hid it are old now. Can’t do it themselves.
Jayne has set his fork down.
He hasn’t looked up from his plate. He is looking at his plate very carefully. But the fork is down.
SAFF

CONTACT  (over comms)
Modest. Job’s the point, not the coin.
Saff looks at Jayne. Jayne is still looking at his plate.
SAFF

The mess room is quiet again. Different quiet than before.
SAFF
Jayne.

JAYNE

*Two — The Moon*

**Dead Moon off Jiangyin — Exterior — Day**
The moon has a name on the charts. Nobody uses it.
It orbits Jiangyin at a distance that suggests an afterthought — close enough to be administratively someone’s problem, far enough that nobody ever got around to caring what that problem was.
The surface is dust and scrub. The particular shade of brown that means this world tried once and gave up. Sky the colour of old iron.
Serenity sits in a flat basin half a mile from the settlement. Steam from the landing struts. The tick of cooling metal.
The ramp comes down.
PIP appears at the top of it. Looks at the sky. Looks at the dust. Looks back at the ship with the expression of a man who has already decided he is staying with the more interesting option.

JAYNE comes down last. Hat on. Vera on his back. One boot on the dust. He stops.

SAFF

SAFF
Jayne.

Saff looks at Cas. Cas shrugs.

**Dead Moon — The Settlement — Continuous**
It is not much of a town. A main street that is more of a suggestion. Low buildings cut from the same pale rock as the ground they sit on. A mining rig on the ridge above — old, still working, turning slow against the sky.
People going about their business. Not many. The kind of population that means everyone knows everyone and has done for twenty years and stopped being interesting to each other a long time ago.
They clock the three of them immediately.
Not hostile. Not welcoming. The particular stillness of people who have learned that strangers mean one of two things and are waiting to find out which.
Jayne walks like he knows where he’s going.
He doesn’t. But he walks like it anyway.
**Dead Moon — The Digger’s Rest — Continuous**
The bar is called the Digger’s Rest. The sign is hand painted. The door is open because the door is always open.
Inside: low light, four tables, a bar that has seen everything and stopped judging. A woman behind it who clocks them the moment they walk in and doesn’t stop what she’s doing.
Three men at the far table. Old. The kind of old that happened outdoors and in poor weather and in situations that didn’t always resolve cleanly.
They look up.
They look at Saff. They look at Cas. They look at Jayne.
They look at Jayne for a little longer than the others.
Jayne pulls out a chair and sits down like he was invited.
JAYNE

*Fourteen — A Week*

**Simon and Kaylee’s Settlement — Various — The Week**
Serenity sits in the flat ground beyond the track and doesn’t move for seven days.
This is not something she has done in a long time. She seems to have no objection to it.
The crew absorbs into the settlement the way crews absorb into places when they have no choice and nowhere else to be. Cas helps with whatever needs flying. Saff helps with whatever needs organising. Pip spends most of his time in the engine room and emerges for meals with a different kind of grease on his arm each day.
Jayne stays in bed because Simon told him to and for once in his life he doesn’t have the energy to argue about it.
